What is a Buyer Persona?
A buyer persona is a semi-fictional representation of the type of person who buys your product. Personas drive every buyer-facing motion: messaging, prospecting, discovery, presentation, and CS onboarding.
ICP
Describes the Company
Which organizations are the best fit — defined by firmographics like size, industry, geography, and tech stack.
Buyer Persona
Describes the Individual
Which people within those organizations are involved in the buying decision — their role, pain, needs, and position in the buying committee.
Key Insight

A B2B deal typically involves 3–8 personas: economic buyer, champion, end user, influencer, evaluator, and gatekeeper. Each requires different messaging, different discovery questions, and a different role in your presentation. Treating all of them the same is one of the most common GTM mistakes.

The Persona Framework
For each persona in your buying committee, document the following fields. Complete one worksheet per persona type — most B2B SaaS companies need 2–4 personas at minimum.
Field Description
Role / Title Their job title and function (e.g., VP of Sales, Head of Revenue Ops). Include common title variants.
Is This the Economic Buyer? Yes / No / Influencer. Identifies whether they control the budget or influence the decision.
Top Challenges What keeps them up at night? What problems do they face in their role day-to-day?
Needs What functional outcome do they need — must-have requirements for a solution to be considered?
Wants What would make them truly delighted — nice-to-have enhancements beyond the baseline requirements?
Success Metrics How are they measured? What does a good year look like for them? Link your solution to their KPIs.
Objections Common objections they raise and how to address them. Every persona has predictable objections — document and prepare for each.
Buying Journey Role Are they an initiator, influencer, decision-maker, user, or gatekeeper? Defines how you engage them at each stage.
Information Sources Where do they learn? Conferences, LinkedIn, peer networks, analyst reports? Informs channel and content strategy.
Example Personas (Generic B2B SaaS)
These three personas represent a typical buying committee for a B2B SaaS sales productivity tool. Adapt for your product category, buyer type, and market.
Persona 1
The Economic Buyer
e.g., VP Sales / CRO
Cares About
Revenue growth, team efficiency, forecast accuracy, hitting quota
Pain
Poor rep productivity, long ramp times, unpredictable forecast
Wants
A clear ROI story, proof from similar companies, fast time-to-value
Top Objection
"We already have a tool for this"
Buying Role
Decision-maker / budget owner
Persona 2
The Champion
e.g., Sales Operations Manager
Cares About
Process efficiency, CRM accuracy, tool adoption, data quality
Pain
Manual processes, inconsistent data, lack of reporting
Wants
A solution that integrates with the existing stack and is easy to implement
Top Objection
"This will take too long to implement"
Buying Role
Influencer / internal champion / evaluator
Persona 3
The End User
e.g., Account Executive
Cares About
Time savings, ease of use, quota attainment
Pain
Admin burden, lack of guidance on next steps, unclear pipeline health
Wants
A tool that actually helps them close more deals without adding complexity
Top Objection
"Another tool to learn"
Buying Role
User / influencer (adoption gatekeeper)
Connecting Personas to Your GTM Playbook
Personas are not a marketing artifact — they are the operating system for your entire buyer-facing motion. Every stage of the GTM funnel should be differentiated by persona.
GTM Motion How Personas Apply
Prospecting Different sequences for each persona — different channels, messaging, and CTAs. An economic buyer requires a different outreach approach than a champion or an end user.
Discovery Different question frameworks by persona. The Economic Buyer cares about ROI and business outcomes; the Champion cares about implementation and workflow fit; the End User cares about ease of use and time savings.
Presentation Different swim lanes based on which personas are in the room. Map each section of your presentation to the persona whose pain it addresses. Adjust dynamically as the room composition changes.
CS Onboarding Different swim lanes based on buyer context. The onboarding process for a champion-led deal differs from one where the economic buyer is highly involved. CS must know who bought and why.
Best Practice

Train every rep and CSM to identify which personas are in the room before every meeting. The ability to read the buyer mix and adjust messaging in real time is one of the highest-leverage skills in enterprise SaaS selling.
