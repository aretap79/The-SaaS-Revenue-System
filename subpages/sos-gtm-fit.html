What is GTM Fit?

GTM Fit is the ability to acquire customers in your ICP profitably and repeatably — at a cost structure that supports sustainable growth. It requires three simultaneous conditions to all be true at once.

Condition 1
Codified GTM Playbook
A documented, repeatable process that any trained rep can execute — not a set of heroic behaviors that only the best reps can replicate.
Condition 2
Scalable Demand Generation Channel
At least one measurable channel that produces consistent, qualified SQLs at a predictable Cost per SQL — with clear line-of-sight to your ICP buyers.
Condition 3
Positive Unit Economics (LIUE > 3)
The leading indicator of LTV/CAC exceeds 3 — meaning the economics of customer acquisition are structurally sustainable at any growth rate you choose.
The Shift from Stage 1 to Stage 2

Stage 1 answers: "Can customers succeed?" Stage 2 answers: "Can we build a machine that acquires customers who succeed?" The question is no longer about product-customer fit — it is about the economics and repeatability of the sales and marketing motion itself.

The LIUE Formula (Leading Indicator of Unit Economics)

LIUE is the core formula of Stage 2. It decomposes the lagging LTV/CAC metric into variables that are trackable in real time — giving you a monthly pulse on GTM Fit 9–12 months before the lagging data would be available.

The Full LIUE Formula
LIUE = LTV / CAC
LTV = ACV × GM% ÷ Annual Churn Rate
CAC = [Cost per SQL ÷ SQL-to-Customer%]  +  [Salesperson Monthly Cost ÷ (SQLs per Salesperson per Month × SQL-to-Customer%)]
LIUE > 3 = GTM Fit achieved. Target: 5+
Below 3 means your GTM motion cannot sustain itself at scale. Between 3 and 5 is acceptable but fragile. 5+ gives you durable, scalable unit economics.
Variable Definitions
ACV
Annual Contract Value
Average annual revenue per customer. Tracks whether deal sizes are consistent with your pricing model — drift in ACV signals discounting or ICP mix shift.
GM%
Gross Margin Percentage
Typically 70–85% for SaaS businesses. The most predictable component of the LIUE formula — rarely changes dramatically in the short term.
Annual Churn Rate
Annual ARR Churn Percentage
% of ARR churned annually. The most destructive variable in the formula — doubling churn cuts LTV in half. LIR achievement from Stage 1 is the primary control lever.
Cost per SQL
Fully Loaded Marketing Cost per SQL
Total demand generation spend (including headcount) divided by number of Sales Qualified Leads. Your primary measure of demand gen efficiency.
SQL-to-Customer%
SQL to Closed-Won Conversion Rate
% of SQLs that become paying customers. Appears in the denominator of both CAC components — small improvements compound dramatically across the entire formula.
Salesperson Monthly Cost
Fully Loaded Rep Cost per Month
Base salary + variable + benefits + tools + management overhead. Apply a 25% overhead multiplier to fully account for management, training, and ramp time.
SQLs per Rep per Month
Rep Productivity Metric
Number of Sales Qualified Leads a rep engages per month. Drives the Salesperson CAC denominator — more SQLs per rep reduces the per-customer cost of the sales team.
LIUE Threshold
GTM Fit Threshold
LIUE > 3 is the minimum threshold. LIUE of 3 means LTV is exactly 3x the cost to acquire — below 3 is structurally unsustainable. Target is 5+ for durable GTM Fit.

Building the GTM Playbook

The GTM Playbook is the complete, documented system for acquiring and retaining ICP customers. In Stage 1 you discovered what works. In Stage 2 you codify it so any trained rep can execute it consistently.

Critical Principle: Build Outside-In

The fatal mistake is designing the GTM playbook "inside-out" — starting with your product features and projecting them onto buyers. The correct approach is "outside-in": start with buyer behavior, buyer journey, and buyer language — then design your sales process to match what buyers actually do and say.

The 5-Component GTM Playbook (+ CS Onboarding)
1 ICP Definition → Foundation for all components
A Green / Yellow / Red framework for qualifying incoming accounts. Green = must engage immediately, all ICP criteria met. Yellow = engage with qualification call. Red = do not engage, outside ICP. Every prospect touched by sales or marketing is assigned a color based on documented firmographic and psychographic criteria. Links to: ICP Worksheet
2 Buyer Journey Map → Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Success
The foundation document. Maps what the buyer experiences at each stage: what they are trying to learn, what triggers their search, what objections they have, and what moves them forward. Every other guide (prospecting, discovery, presentation, CS) is designed to match one stage of this journey. Never designed from the seller's perspective — always from the buyer's.
3 Prospecting Guide → Awareness stage
How reps initiate conversations with ICP buyers who are not yet actively evaluating. Includes: multi-touch sequence design (6+ attempts required for 90% contact rate), message templates matched to awareness-stage buyer language, channel mix (email, phone, LinkedIn, video), and personalization framework. Key rule: vary both message and medium across attempts — never repeat the same message through the same channel. Prospecting Guide Template →
4 Discovery Guide → Consideration stage
The structured question framework reps use to uncover pain, urgency, and fit. Includes: opening questions (challenge or goal framing), situation questions (status quo), implication questions (cost of the problem), and vision questions (desired outcome). Goal: rep talks <50% of the time. Great discovery is the foundation of every great demo and every successful onboarding. Discovery Guide Template →
5 Presentation Guide → Decision stage (3–5 swim lanes)
3–5 swim lanes based on the buyer context discovered during discovery. The presentation lane is selected based on the primary pain, use case, or buyer persona — not randomly. Each lane follows a consistent sequence: recap buyer context → share company vision → illustrate capability → present case study → discuss price and integration → ask for questions → recap and ask for the business. Never give the same demo twice. Presentation Guide Template →
CS CS Onboarding Guide → Success stage (3–5 swim lanes matching presentation)
Mirrors the 3–5 presentation swim lanes to ensure continuity from the sales experience to onboarding. Biggest mistake: starting onboarding from scratch, ignoring what the rep learned in discovery and presentation. The CS onboarding guide should begin with a recap of buyer context and buyer-verified success criteria — not a generic onboarding checklist. Oriented entirely around driving customers to LIR achievement as fast as possible. CS Onboarding Guide Template →
Film Reviews: The Stage 2 Cadence
Film Reviews — Daily During GTM Fit Phase

Listen to recorded discovery and presentation calls together as a team. Assign one person to identify what worked and one to identify what to improve. Rotate the "hot seat" — each rep presents a recent call weekly. Include marketing, product, and CS in the review — not just sales. Film Reviews are the primary mechanism for updating the GTM Playbook in real time based on actual customer conversations. The playbook is a living document — updated weekly, not quarterly.

Demand Generation in Stage 2

In Stage 1, demand came from personal networks and referrals. In Stage 2, the goal is to prove that one scalable, measurable demand generation channel can produce ICP buyers at a predictable Cost per SQL.

Stage 1 vs. Stage 2 Demand Gen
Dimension Stage 1: PMF Stage 2: GTM Fit
Source Personal network, warm referrals One proven, scalable inbound or outbound channel
Volume Small — enough to build cohort data Consistent — enough to hit LIUE model targets monthly
Measurability Informal — relationships, not metrics Fully instrumented — Cost per SQL tracked from day one
ICP precision Learning ICP from early cohorts ICP is validated — channel targets it explicitly
Number of channels Zero or one (whatever generates referrals) One — prove it before diversifying
4 Criteria for Selecting Your Stage 2 Demand Gen Channel
  1. Alignment: Your ICP buyers must be active on this channel. If you're targeting VP-level buyers at mid-market companies, a LinkedIn outbound campaign is more aligned than an SEO blog. Match channel to buyer behavior, not to what's cheapest or easiest.
  2. Measurability: You must be able to track the exact Cost per SQL from this channel from day one. Channels with long or opaque attribution paths (e.g., brand awareness) cannot be used to track LIUE denominator inputs in real time.
  3. Rapid test: You should be able to run a 30–60 day test that provides enough signal to make a go/no-go decision. Channels with 6+ month feedback loops (content SEO, event marketing) are difficult to optimize during the GTM Fit phase.
  4. Customer success alignment: Success for the demand gen channel is measured by LIR achievement rate of customers sourced from it — not just lead conversion. A channel that generates non-ICP buyers with low LIR achievement is a negative-LIUE channel even if conversion looks attractive.
Common Stage 2 Demand Gen Channels
  • Outbound cold email + LinkedIn: High ICP precision, fully measurable, rapid to test and iterate
  • Content SEO: Long feedback loop but compounds over time — better for companies with 6+ month runway in Stage 2
  • Webinars and virtual events: High engagement, measurable registrant-to-SQL pipeline, strong for mid-market buyers
  • Partner referrals: High-quality ICP leads with warm introduction — often the most efficient CAC source available
  • Field events: Strong for enterprise buyers — high cost but high signal quality; measure fully-loaded cost per SQL including event overhead
One Channel First

Diversify into a second demand gen channel only in Stage 3. In Stage 2, split demand gen attention across multiple channels before any single channel is proven produces inflated Cost per SQL across all of them. Prove one channel works — then add the second.

Pricing in Stage 2: Solve for Unit Economics

Pricing in Stage 2 shifts from "solve for customer commitment" (Stage 1) to "solve for unit economics." The question is no longer whether customers will implement — it is whether your price point supports LIUE > 3.

Three Pricing Lenses
Lens 1
Buyer ROI
Quantify the economic value your product delivers to the buyer. Price at 30–40% of that value. This leaves ROI clearly on the table (buyer keeps 60–70%), makes the renewal decision easy, and creates a value-based price anchor rather than a cost-based one.
Lens 2
Unit Economics
Work backwards from the LIUE formula. What ACV is required to achieve LIUE > 3 given your current CAC structure? If list-price ACV does not support LIUE > 3 without discounting, your pricing model must change before you can scale.
Lens 3
Competition
Understand the competitive pricing landscape. Do not race to the bottom — competing on price invites commoditization and destroys the unit economics you are trying to prove. Win on value differentiation, not discounting.
Packaging Shifts in Stage 2
  • Move from custom/flexible to standardized tiers: custom packaging creates deal complexity, extends sales cycles, and makes it impossible to compare deal economics across cohorts
  • Annual contracts only: strong discounts for multi-year commitments — quarterly or monthly billing options should carry a meaningful premium
  • Introduce usage-based components where appropriate: usage-based pricing creates natural NRR expansion pathways and aligns price with customer value realization
  • Eliminate deep discounting: if unit economics are only positive with heavy discounts, the list price needs to be raised — not the discount normalized
The Sales Hire in Stage 2: Process Builder

The Stage 1 PM+AE hybrid who discovered the playbook must now be replaced — or supplemented — by someone who can take a proven playbook and codify, train, and scale it. This is a different skill set.

Stage 1 — What You Had
PM + AE Hybrid
Discovers the playbook. High product intuition, comfortable with ambiguity, exploratory mindset. Closes deals through deep customer knowledge and personal relationships. Cannot be replicated at scale — that's the point.
Stage 2 — What You Need
Process Builder
Codifies and scales the playbook. Prior experience leading playbook-driven teams. Data-oriented: measures everything. Strong coaching mindset: develops reps through Film Reviews, not heroics. Comfortable with process discipline. This is NOT the closer-hero who wins through personal charm.
Process Builder Hiring Process
  • Use a defined scorecard with 5–10 explicitly weighted attributes — link to: Sales Leader Scorecard and Sales Rep Scorecard
  • Reference checks with former direct reports are mandatory — not just former managers. Ask: "Did this person build process or rely on personal talent?"
  • Audition the hire: give them 30 days to present a Film Review analysis of 5 recorded calls before making the offer final
  • Scale pace: 2 salespeople per month maximum during the GTM Fit phase — never hire a large cohort at once
  • Hire only after the Process Builder has proven the playbook yields LIUE approaching threshold — not before
Critical Gate

Do NOT scale the sales team to more than 2–4 reps until the Process Builder has proven the GTM Playbook produces LIUE approaching threshold with at least 2–3 reps executing it. Scaling before this is the most common Series A-to-B failure mode.

Compensation in Stage 2: Retention + Unit Economics

Stage 2 compensation preserves the 50/50 structure from Stage 1 — and adds a unit economics health component to further align rep behavior with the LIUE target.

50%
At Contract Signing
Paid at close — rewards closed revenue
50%
At LIR Achievement
Paid when customer achieves E within T — rewards customer success
Stage 2 Comp Additions
  • LIUE health component: if a deal-level unit economic analysis shows poor economics (large discount, complex deal, outsized CS requirement), reduce commission proportionally — not a simple binary, but a gradient modifier
  • Quota-setting formula: derive from the LIUE denominator — SQLs per rep per month × SQL-to-customer rate × ACV. Quota should be the output of the unit economics model, not a top-down revenue number
  • Avoid SPIFs and exceptions: every SPIF and comp exception signals that the base plan is broken — fix the plan rather than papering over it with bonuses
  • Full transparency: every rep should be able to calculate their own exact commission at any point in the month with no ambiguity — opaque comp plans create distrust and behavioral noise
Biggest Compensation Mistake
Delegating comp design to the head of sales, who copies the comp plan from their last employer — which was designed for a different business model, different stage, and different retention economics. Sales comp must be derived from the LIUE formula and the LIR definition — not imported from a prior company.
The GTM Fit Speedometer

GTM Fit requires two simultaneous green lights — LIUE and LIR must both be on-target at the same time. A single green light does not mean GTM Fit. Both must be green, and both must stay green as you scale.

✓ Green Light
LIUE > 3
Leading Indicator of Unit Economics is above threshold. Your GTM motion is generating customers at a cost that supports sustainable growth. Track weekly by cohort. Target LIUE > 3 for 2 consecutive quarters.
✓ Green Light
LIR Achievement ≥ P%
Leading Indicator of Retention is still meeting threshold. Customers acquired through the Stage 2 GTM motion are still achieving the LIR event at the same rate as Stage 1 cohorts. If LIR falls as you scale, you are closing bad-fit customers.
Red Flag Pattern — Act Immediately

LIUE rising but LIR falling means you are acquiring customers faster and more efficiently — but those customers are not succeeding. You are closing bad-fit deals quickly. Churn will compound and destroy unit economics 12 months from now. Stop scaling acquisition and diagnose the ICP drift before continuing.

LIUE Dashboard Rules
  • Track LIUE weekly, broken down by cohort — not just as a company-wide average
  • The blue line (actual) must stay above the red line (target) on all four LIUE component metrics: SQLs per rep, Cost per SQL, SQL-to-customer%, and ACV
  • If any metric drops below target for 2+ consecutive months, diagnose and address the root cause before it compounds into unit economic deterioration
  • Look at SQL-to-customer% data that is 3–6 months old for accuracy — recent cohorts are still in the pipeline and will show artificially low conversion rates
When to Declare GTM Fit and Move to Stage 3

GTM Fit is a convergence of six conditions — not a single metric crossing a threshold. All six must be simultaneously true before moving to Stage 3 (Growth & Moat).

  • ✓ LIUE > 3 for 2 consecutive quarters — the leading indicator of LTV/CAC has been above threshold for 6+ months, not just a momentary spike
  • ✓ LIR achievement rate ≥ threshold for 2 consecutive quarters — customer success is still being maintained at Stage 1 levels despite scaled acquisition, confirming ICP discipline is holding
  • ✓ GTM Playbook is fully codified and being executed consistently by all reps — not just the best reps, and not just during Film Review week. Every rep, every week, following the same documented process.
  • ✓ Demand gen channel is generating consistent, measurable SQLs — the channel has been running for 3+ months with a stable Cost per SQL and a predictable SQL volume that can be modeled in the LIUE formula
  • ✓ Sales hiring and ramp model is validated — you can predict how long it takes a new rep to ramp to 100% quota attainment, and that ramp model is consistently accurate across 2+ rep hire cohorts
  • ✓ Pricing is standardized and unit economics are positive at list price — not at a discounted price. If LIUE > 3 requires discounting below list to close deals, the pricing model is broken and must be fixed before Stage 3
Key Metrics in Stage 2

Seven metrics define Stage 2 health. LIUE is the primary operating target. LIR Achievement Rate is the retained Stage 1 constraint — it must never be allowed to fall as you scale acquisition.

Metric Definition Target Why It Matters
LIUE LTV divided by CAC, using fully decomposed formula >3 (target 5+) Core GTM Fit measure — the unit economics goal the entire Stage 2 motion is optimized toward
SQL-to-Customer% % of Sales Qualified Leads converting to closed-won customers Varies by segment (typically 15–30%) CAC denominator driver — appears in both demand gen and salesperson CAC components simultaneously
Cost per SQL Fully loaded marketing and SDR cost per SQL generated Minimize — derived from LIUE model Primary demand gen efficiency metric — must be tracked from first day a new channel is activated
Sales Cycle Length Average days from SQL to closed-won <90 days for mid-market Velocity metric — longer cycles increase salesperson CAC and reduce SQLs-per-rep throughput
Ramp Time to Quota Average months for a new AE to reach 100% quota attainment <6 months for mid-market Hiring model input — must be predictable and consistent before scaling headcount
LIR Achievement Rate % of new customers achieving LIR event (E) within T days ≥60–80% (same threshold as Stage 1) Retention anchor — must not fall as acquisition scales. A falling LIR rate is the earliest warning of ICP drift or playbook degradation
NRR Net Revenue Retention — ARR retained + expanded minus churned, from existing customers >100% Proof of unit economics in action — NRR > 100% means the customer base grows even without any new logos, which structurally improves LTV/CAC
In the 6-Phase Revenue System

Stage 2 (GTM Fit) is the operational mandate for Phase 3 and Phase 4. Every demand gen decision, funnel design, and rep productivity metric in those phases directly determines your LIUE.

Phase 3 — Primary Home
GTM, Demand Gen & Segmentation
Stage 2 demand gen work: one scalable channel, 4 selection criteria, LIUE-positive cost per SQL, LIR-aligned channel (channel SQLs must hit LIR threshold).
Phase 4 — Execution Layer
Pipeline, MEDDICC & Forecasting
SQL-to-customer% and rep productivity from Phase 4 are direct LIUE CAC inputs. Improving win rate and prospecting discipline directly improves LIUE.
Phase 2 — Hiring Mode
2 Reps/Month Pace
Once LIUE > 3, begin the 2-per-month hiring pace. Hire Process Builders. Comp adds LIUE health component. Iterate scorecard at month 6.
Phase 6 — If GTM Fit Fails
LIUE Diagnostic
LIUE below 3? Identify: LTV side (churn, ACV, GM%) vs. CAC side (cost/SQL, SQL-to-customer%, rep productivity). Fix the broken lever before adding headcount.
