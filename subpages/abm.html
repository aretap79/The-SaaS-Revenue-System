What ABM Is and Why It Emerged

Account-Based Marketing (ABM) is a B2B go-to-market strategy in which marketing and sales treat a specific set of accounts as a market of one — tailoring content, outreach, advertising, and sales plays to the individual needs and context of each target account, rather than broadcasting generic campaigns to the full addressable market.

ABM emerged as a response to the limitations of traditional demand generation in enterprise SaaS. As buyers became more sophisticated and more resistant to generic outreach, as buying committees grew from 2 to 6-10 stakeholders, and as deal cycles extended from weeks to months, the economics of spray-and-pray demand generation collapsed. Generating 10,000 MQLs means nothing if 9,800 of them are too small, wrong industry, or already using a competitor.

The Core ABM Shift

Traditional demand gen asks: "How do we get more leads?" ABM asks: "How do we get deeper engagement inside the 200 accounts that represent 80% of our revenue potential?" The shift is from volume to precision — fewer targets, more investment per target, higher close rates.

The 3 ABM Tiers

Not all target accounts deserve the same investment. The ABM tiering model matches the depth of personalization and resource investment to the revenue potential of each account.

Tier 1
1:1 Strategic ABM
5–50 accounts / AE or named segment
Maximum personalization. Dedicated content, custom microsites, bespoke executive events, named AE + SDR + CSM pod assigned. Every touchpoint references the account's specific context — their industry, their stack, their org chart, their stated pain.
  • Custom account-specific landing pages
  • Personalized direct mail packages
  • Executive briefing center invitations
  • Customized ROI models with their own numbers
  • Named research reports addressing their industry
Tier 2
1:Few Programmatic ABM
50–500 accounts / cluster
Cluster-level personalization. Accounts are grouped by industry, use case, or company profile and receive content tailored to the cluster — not the individual company, but not generic either. Automated personalization at scale using Demandbase, 6sense, or Terminus.
  • Industry/vertical-specific landing pages
  • Cluster-targeted LinkedIn ad campaigns
  • Programmatic display to target account IPs
  • Personalized email sequences by persona + industry
  • Industry-specific case studies and proof points
Tier 3
1:Many ABM at Scale
500–5,000 accounts
Persona-level personalization at scale. This is the ABM bridge to traditional demand gen — targeting a broad account list with messaging aligned to ICP persona, using intent data to prioritize which accounts to serve ads and outreach to in any given week. Technology-dependent.
  • Intent data-triggered ad serving (Bombora topics)
  • 6sense / Demandbase predictive scoring
  • LinkedIn Matched Audiences — contact-level targeting
  • Behavioral personalization on website
  • Automated SDR sequences triggered by intent signals

Building the Target Account List (TAL)

The TAL is the foundation of your ABM program. A poorly constructed TAL — one that includes accounts that are too small, wrong vertical, or already lost — wastes every downstream investment. TAL construction is a joint marketing + sales process, run quarterly.

  1. Start with ICP scoring Apply your ICP criteria (firmographic: company size, industry, geography, tech stack; technographic: tools they use that integrate with yours; behavioral: previous engagement with your content or trials) to your total addressable market. Score each account 0-100 against ICP fit.
  2. Overlay intent data Identify which ICP-fit accounts are currently researching your category. A company that scores 85 on ICP fit AND is currently showing 3rd-party intent signals for topics related to your solution is 5-10x more likely to buy in the next 90 days than one that is not in-market. Prioritize these accounts for Tier 1 and Tier 2.
  3. Get sales input Have each AE review their account list and flag: (a) strategic accounts they want to prioritize regardless of intent signal, (b) accounts with existing relationships or previous contact history, (c) accounts to exclude (current customers, partnership discussions, competitive situations). Sales must own the TAL — if they don't, they won't work the accounts marketing targets.
  4. Assign tiers Tier 1: highest ICP score + active intent + AE strategic priority. Tier 2: strong ICP fit, moderate intent, available SDR capacity. Tier 3: ICP fit, no active intent, technology-led nurture.
  5. Establish TAL review cadence Quarterly TAL refresh: remove accounts that have gone 180+ days with zero engagement. Add new accounts that have entered intent. Promote/demote accounts between tiers based on engagement progression.

Intent Data Platforms

3rd-Party Intent
Bombora
Tracks topic research across a consortium of B2B content publishers. Reports when a company's employees are consuming content about topics relevant to your category at above-baseline rates. Best for early-stage intent before they visit your website.
Predictive AI
6sense
Uses ML to predict which accounts are in each buying stage (awareness → consideration → decision). Combines 1st-party, 3rd-party intent, and CRM data. Particularly strong for B2B SaaS with complex sales cycles. Activates targeted ads to predicted in-market accounts.
Review Platform
G2 Buyer Intent
Identifies companies whose employees are viewing your G2 profile, your competitors' G2 profiles, or G2 comparison pages for your category. The highest-signal intent data available — a company actively comparing you to competitors on G2 is already in an active evaluation.
ABM Platform
Demandbase
End-to-end ABM platform combining account identification (IP-based), intent data, website personalization, and targeted advertising. Good for larger marketing teams running full Tier 1-3 ABM programs with unified analytics across channels.
Social
LinkedIn Matched Audiences
Upload your TAL company names (or contact emails) and serve LinkedIn ads specifically to people at those companies with targeting by title/role. The only channel that can reach a specific person at a specific company by title — the VP of Engineering at Acme Corp — without needing their contact info.
ABM Platform
Terminus
ABM platform strong in display advertising orchestration and email signature marketing. Good for companies already using HubSpot and wanting a tighter CRM-to-ABM loop. Often chosen for mid-market ABM at Tier 2-3 scale.

The Coordinated ABM Play

The defining difference between ABM done right and ABM done wrong is coordination. Marketing running ads to an account while the SDR sends cold emails and the AE calls the CFO blind — without any of them knowing what the others are doing — is not ABM. It is expensive chaos. Real ABM requires a synchronized, orchestrated play across marketing, SDR, and AE against the same account at the same time.

RevOps / Marketing
Week 0: Account enters TAL — intent signal detected
6sense / Bombora signals that Acme Corp has surged on topics related to your category. Account is promoted to Tier 1. AE is notified. Account enrichment is run (new stakeholders found via LinkedIn Sales Navigator + ZoomInfo). CRM account record is updated.
Marketing
Week 1–2: Surround with relevant content
LinkedIn Matched Audiences campaign activated for all employees at Acme Corp, with ads referencing Acme's industry and pain. IP-targeted display ads served to Acme's office IP ranges. Personalized website experience activated — Acme's industry vertical is detected, homepage swaps messaging to their use case.
SDR
Week 2–3: Warm outreach to economic buyer + champion
SDR reaches out to 2-3 stakeholders at Acme with personalized messages that reference their specific context — not a generic sequence. Email 1 references a recent press release or LinkedIn post from the target. Call 2 references a case study from their exact vertical. Not a blast — a conversation.
AE
Week 3–4: Executive outreach and relationship activation
AE reaches out to VP+ stakeholders with a well-researched, peer-to-peer message. If there are mutual connections or board-level relationships, now is the time to activate them. AE reviews all intent and engagement data from prior 3 weeks before any outreach.
Marketing + AE
Week 4–8: Personalized content delivery
Custom landing page goes live for Acme referencing their industry benchmarks. Industry-specific ROI calculator is sent. If Tier 1, an executive briefing invitation is extended. All touchpoints reference earlier conversations — every interaction builds on the previous one.
AE + SDR
Week 6–12: Discovery meeting achieved — ABM play has worked
A discovery call is booked. The AE arrives having read every intent signal, every piece of engagement data, every LinkedIn post by the stakeholders. The prospect feels known, not targeted. This is the payoff of coordinated ABM.

ABM ROI: Why the Math Works

ABM ROI Comparison
Traditional demand gen: 10,000 MQLs × $150 CPL = $1.5M spend → 2% MQL-to-Opp × $50K ACV = $10M pipeline
ABM: 200 Tier 1 accounts × $5,000 account investment = $1M spend → 30% account-to-opp × $150K ACV (larger deals) = $9M pipeline at lower spend, with higher win rate and shorter sales cycle

The ABM model wins on efficiency — fewer accounts, fewer reps distracted by poor-fit leads, higher ACV per opportunity, and better win rates because reps arrive at every meeting better prepared. The challenge is that ABM results take 6-12 months to fully materialize, which creates pressure from leadership expecting MQL volume metrics in months 1-3.

ABM Failure Modes

Marketing runs ABM without sales commitment
Marketing invests in TAL-targeted ads and content. Sales ignores the signals and keeps their own pipeline lists. Result: expensive campaigns with no follow-through. ABM only works as a joint program with shared accountability.
TAL is too large and too generic
"Let's put 5,000 accounts in Tier 1" — this defeats the purpose. If you can't personalize at Tier 1 depth, those accounts are Tier 3 at best. Over-large TALs dilute investment and produce Tier 3 results at Tier 1 costs.
Measuring ABM success with demand gen metrics
Reporting ABM performance as MQL volume is a category error. ABM must be measured by account engagement, multi-thread %, and pipeline from target accounts. Leadership that insists on MQL volume will starve ABM before it can work.
Personalization stops at the company name in the subject line
"Hi [First Name], I noticed [Company] is growing fast..." is not ABM — it is mail-merge. Real personalization requires researching the company's specific priorities, referencing their public statements, and connecting your solution to their stated goals. This takes time and cannot be fully automated.
No feedback loop on account engagement data to sales
If the AE doesn't know that the VP of Engineering at Acme Corp visited your pricing page 3 times last week, viewed a case study, and engaged with a LinkedIn ad — they cannot use that intelligence. Engagement data must flow from marketing to CRM to rep in real time.
