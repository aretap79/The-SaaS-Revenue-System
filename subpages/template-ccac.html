CCAC Model — Cohort Customer Acquisition Cost

Unlike traditional CAC, CCAC tracks acquisition cost by cohort over time — revealing whether your GTM efficiency is improving or deteriorating as you scale.

Section 1 — What is CCAC?

CCAC (Cohort Customer Acquisition Cost) is the cohort-based version of the standard CAC metric. Where traditional CAC averages all customers together, CCAC shows acquisition cost period by period — making trends visible.

Definition
CCAC = Total S&M Spend in Period ÷ Logos Acquired in Period
Unlike standard CAC (which divides all-time S&M spend by all-time customer count), CCAC is computed separately for each month or quarter. This transforms a single snapshot metric into a trendline — which is where the insight lives.
Why It Matters

Reveals whether CAC is improving, declining, or stable as you scale. A rising CAC that isn't visible in the aggregate number is often the first sign that GTM efficiency is eroding.

When Scale Helps

If CCAC is declining over time, your GTM motion is becoming more efficient as you scale — word of mouth, brand, and rep productivity are all compounding.

When Scale Hurts

If CCAC is rising, you are spending more to acquire each new customer. This often precedes a unit economics crisis — catch it here before it shows up in a funding conversation.

Section 2 — How to Calculate CCAC

Inputs

Total S&M spend (all sales + marketing headcount + program costs) New logos acquired in that period
S&M Spend Definition

Include: all sales headcount costs (base + OTE), all marketing headcount costs, demand gen program spend (paid search, events, content), SDR tooling, and direct sales tools (ZoomInfo, Outreach, etc.). Exclude: product costs, CS headcount, and support tooling.

Positive CCAC Trend
CCAC declining from $750 to $555 over 6 months (−26%) with relatively flat S&M spend means logos acquired are increasing faster than spend — a textbook healthy GTM efficiency improvement. Rep productivity, pipeline quality, or conversion rates are improving as the motion matures.
Negative CCAC Trend — Investigate
If CCAC were rising despite consistent spend, investigate: Are rep activity levels dropping? Are demand gen conversion rates declining? Is the ICP definition drifting (selling to harder-to-close accounts)? Is ramp time extending? CCAC deterioration is a lagging indicator of a problem that started 3–6 months earlier.

Section 3 — CCAC vs. Traditional CAC

Traditional CAC gives you a snapshot; CCAC gives you a trend. In most business decisions, trends are more actionable than snapshots.

Dimension CCAC (Cohort) Traditional CAC
Calculation S&M spend in period ÷ logos in period Total S&M (all time) ÷ total customers (all time)
Shows trend? ✓ Yes — period over period ✗ No — single blended number
Detects early deterioration? ✓ Yes — visible within 1–2 months ✗ Masked by historical averages
Useful for board presentations? ✓ Shows efficiency trajectory Partial — only a snapshot, not a story
Useful for comp design? ✓ Can reward behavior that reduces CCAC ✗ Too blended to drive specific behavior
When most valuable Scaling phases — when headcount + spend is growing Early stage when cohort data is insufficient
Use Both

Traditional CAC remains useful for payback period calculations and benchmarking against industry data. CCAC is the operational metric — use it to manage the business. Report both to the board, with CCAC as the primary efficiency trendline.

Section 4 — Using CCAC in Board Presentations

Present CCAC as part of a three-chart package that together answers whether you are scaling in a healthy, sustainable way.

01
LIR Cohort Chart
Are new customers retaining at the rate that validates product-market fit?
02
LIUE Dashboard
Are the leading indicators of unit economics (pipeline, productivity, conversion) trending in the right direction?
03
CCAC Trend
Is the cost to acquire each new customer going up or down as we scale?
Together, these three charts answer one question
"Are we scaling in a healthy, sustainable way?"
If LIR is holding, LIUE is improving, and CCAC is declining — you have a board-level proof of scalable, healthy growth. If any one of these is deteriorating, you have an early warning signal that something in the GTM system needs diagnosis.
Board Presentation Framing

Don't present CCAC in isolation. Frame it as "As we have scaled from X to Y customers, our acquisition cost per new logo has declined from $X to $Y — a Z% improvement. This reflects improving rep productivity, better pipeline quality from demand gen maturation, and a tightening ICP definition."
