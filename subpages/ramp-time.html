Definition and Why It Matters to Revenue Capacity

Definition

Ramp Time to Quota is the number of months from a sales rep's start date until they consistently achieve 100% of their full quota target. It is not the time until they close their first deal — it is the time until they are fully productive contributors to the revenue plan. A rep on a 6-month ramp is expected to close roughly 50% of their full quota in month 3, 75% in month 5, and 100% from month 7 onward (the exact curve depends on your ramp structure).

Ramp time matters to revenue capacity because every new hire is a negative revenue event for the first N months of their tenure. They consume salary, benefits, management time, and enablement resources without producing equivalent revenue. A company that plans to hit its revenue target by adding 10 reps in Q1 — but uses a 6-month ramp model and adds those reps in January — will not see full productivity until July. If the revenue target starts in January, there is a 6-month gap that must be filled by existing reps or existing expansion — which is frequently not modeled.

The Ramp Efficiency Formula

Ramp Efficiency
Ramp Efficiency(month N) = Actual Quota Attainment at Month N / Expected Full Quota
Example: If quota is $100K/quarter and a rep closes $30K in month 3, their 3-month efficiency is 30%. Track at months 3, 6, 9, and 12 for a complete ramp curve. Compare cohort of new hires from the same quarter — wide variance between them is a management or ICP signal.
Fully-Loaded Ramp Cost Formula
Ramp Cost = (Base Salary × Ramp Months) + OTE Variable (not earned during ramp) + Enablement Investment + Manager Time Cost
Example: $80K base, 6-month ramp, $40K OTE variable (not earned at 0% quota), $10K enablement cost, 20% of $180K manager salary for 6 months ($18K). Total ramp cost: $40K + $40K + $10K + $18K = $108K per rep before they're fully productive. This is the cost of each month of ramp delay you can eliminate.

Benchmarks by Segment

SMB AE
2–4 mo
Short sales cycles mean reps see real deal outcomes quickly. Product simplicity and template-driven demos compress learning.
Mid-Market AE
4–6 mo
Longer cycles and more complex discovery extend ramp. Playbook quality is the primary differentiator between 4-month and 6-month ramps.
Enterprise AE
6–9 mo
Deals take 6-12 months — reps need time to build accounts before they can close. First enterprise close often arrives at month 8-10.
SDR / BDR
2–3 mo
Activity-based — faster feedback loop. First qualified meetings booked by week 3-4. Full meeting quota expected by month 3.
Sales Manager
6–9 mo
Managers ramp their teams, not themselves. Ramp is measured by team performance improvement, not personal quota.
CSM
3–5 mo
CS ramp measured by accounts independently managed and health score improvement. First renewal managed solo typically at month 4-5.

The Hiring Lead Time Math

Worked Example — Hiring Calendar From Revenue Target
Revenue Target
Need 5 additional fully-productive Mid-Market AEs contributing $500K each by Q4
Ramp Time
Mid-Market ramp = 5 months to 75% quota; 7 months to full quota
Time to Hire
Average time from job req open to first day = 8 weeks (2 months)
Total Time: Req to Full Productivity
2 months hiring + 7 months ramp = 9 months total
If Q4 = October, working backward 9 months = January. You must open the job req in January to have fully productive reps in October.
→ To have 5 productive Mid-Market AEs by Q4, you must start hiring in January — not Q3.

This math is the source of the "hockey stick problem" — companies wait until Q2-Q3 to hire because they're not sure they need the headcount yet, then wonder why Q3 and Q4 miss plan despite hiring aggressively. The ramp lag means hiring decisions must be made 2-3 quarters before the revenue is needed.

How to Reduce Ramp Time

Impact: High | Timeline: Immediate
Tighten Your ICP — Before They Arrive
Reps with a narrow, clear ICP ramp faster because they can pattern-match immediately. If your ICP is "mid-market companies with CRMs," a new rep will take 9 months to learn who to call. If it is "Salesforce-using SaaS companies, $20M-$100M ARR, Series B-C, expanding sales team," a rep can prospect effectively by week 3.
Impact: High | Timeline: Weeks
Structured 30/60/90 Onboarding Program
Day 1 through week 8 must be highly structured: product certification by week 2, persona guide mastery by week 3, first demo certification by week 4, first prospecting calls by week 5. Unstructured onboarding ("here's your laptop, shadow some calls") extends ramp by 2-3 months.
Impact: Very High | Timeline: Months
Peer Mentorship + Co-Selling
Pair every new rep with your top-performing AE for the first 60 days of deal work. Not as an observer — as a co-seller who participates. The new rep absorbs pattern recognition that cannot be trained in a classroom. This single investment reduces ramp by 1-2 months.
Impact: Medium | Timeline: Weeks
Give Reps Warm Pipe on Day 1
New reps who inherit zero pipeline spend months 1-3 doing exclusively outbound prospecting — and see no deal outcomes until month 4-5. Give new reps a starter pipeline of 3-5 qualified opportunities from the SDR team or from manager-worked accounts. First close in month 2-3 dramatically improves confidence and learning speed.
Impact: Medium | Timeline: Months
Playbook Quality and Accessibility
A documented, specific, validated sales playbook (discovery questions, objection handlers, competitive positioning, demo structure, champion-building scripts) that a new rep can follow from day 1 dramatically compresses the time to "knowing what good looks like." See Sales Playbook page.
Impact: High | Timeline: Ongoing
Manager Deliberate Coaching Investment
The manager is the single highest-leverage ramp accelerator. A manager who does 2 call-shadow sessions per week with a ramping rep, provides immediate specific feedback, and meets for a 1-hour 1:1 weekly will ramp that rep 30-40% faster than a manager who does monthly check-ins.

The ICP-Playbook-Management Triangle

Ramp time is the output of three inputs working in combination. Each can be improved independently, but all three must be at a minimum quality level for ramp to be fast. If any one of the three is broken, the other two cannot compensate:
