The Two Win Rate Formulas

Win rate is calculated two ways, and both are necessary. Using only one creates blind spots.

Formula 1: Win Rate vs. Active Competition
Won / (Won + Competitive Losses)
Measures your ability to defeat competing vendors when they are present in the deal. Excludes "no decision" outcomes. This is the purest measure of your competitive positioning and sales execution quality. A 40% rate means you win 40% of the time when a competitor is actively working the account.
Formula 2: Win Rate vs. All Opportunities
Won / (Won + All Losses incl. No Decision)
Measures your ability to convert all pipeline — including deals lost to "no decision" (status quo) and deals that stalled. This is the metric that drives pipeline coverage requirements. A lower overall win rate than competitive win rate indicates a do-nothing problem, not a competitive problem.
When to Use Each

Use Formula 1 (vs. competition only) to assess your messaging effectiveness, competitive positioning quality, and battle card relevance. Use Formula 2 (all opportunities) for pipeline coverage planning and capacity modeling. A company that wins 55% of competitive deals but only 20% of all deals has a do-nothing problem — most opportunities never progress to a real competitive decision.

How to Segment Win Rate for Diagnostics

Segmentation DimensionWhat It RevealsDiagnostic Question
By RepPerformance variance across the team. Identify top performers to replicate and bottom performers to coach. 2x+ variance between best and worst is an ICP, training, or management problem.Are top reps winning differently? What specific behaviors predict high win rate?
By Market Segment (SMB/MM/Ent)Where your motion works best. If Enterprise win rate is <10%, you may be mis-investing in that segment relative to your competitive position.Are we investing headcount and resources in the segment where our win rate justifies the investment?
By Lead SourceMarketing-sourced vs. outbound-sourced vs. partner-sourced vs. expansion. Win rates often differ 15-30 points between sources.Which source produces the highest-quality pipeline? Is our investment aligned to source win rates?
By CompetitorWhere you win and lose in competitive situations. A declining win rate against a specific competitor is a signal they've improved their product, messaging, or pricing.What changed in our competitive position against [X] in the last two quarters?
By Deal Size BandWin rate usually declines as deal size increases (more scrutiny, more competition, more stakeholders). Knowing the break point helps sales focus on the right deals.At what ACV threshold does our win rate fall below acceptable? Are we over-investing above that threshold?
By Industry VerticalWin rates in your core ICP verticals vs. adjacent verticals. Reveals where your messaging resonates and where it doesn't.Are we winning in the industries we claim are our ICP? If not, is the ICP wrong or the messaging wrong?
By Sales Cycle LengthWin rate for deals that close quickly vs. deals with extended cycles. Long cycles often indicate misaligned deals — and often predict losses.What is the correlation between sales cycle length and win rate? Is there a threshold beyond which most deals are lost?

What Drives Win Rate Up and Down

Forces That Increase Win Rate
  • Tighter ICP enforcement — pursuing fewer but better-fit deals
  • Champion building — 2+ internal advocates in the deal
  • Executive alignment early in the deal (AE accesses EB by Stage 2)
  • Strong competitive positioning — updated battle cards, effective reframes
  • Reference-led selling — prospect speaks to a reference in their exact vertical
  • Proactive mutual close plan — creates decision momentum
  • Product-led motion — prospect has used the product (PQA pipeline)
  • Improved discovery — reps link pain to quantified business outcomes
  • Rep tenure and experience — win rate typically improves through Year 2-3
  • Enablement investment — updated objection handlers, ROI calculators
Forces That Decrease Win Rate
  • Loose ICP — pursuing accounts that can't buy or won't use the product well
  • Single-threaded deals — one contact, no champions, no EB access
  • Late EB engagement — discovering budget/authority issues at Stage 3-4
  • Competitor improves product or pricing without your team knowing
  • New reps ramping — natural win rate trough in months 1-4
  • Market commoditization — more competitors entering your category
  • Outdated enablement — reps using stale positioning from 2 years ago
  • Proof deficit — not enough relevant case studies or references in new verticals
  • Pricing misalignment — ACV expectations out of step with market
  • Rep attrition — losing high-win-rate reps, replacing with new reps

The Win Rate / Sales Cycle Trade-Off

Prioritize Win Rate
Aggressive qualification — only pursue deals with high ICP fit and active champion. Refuse deals without EB access. Win more of fewer deals. Result: higher win rate, lower pipeline volume needed, longer average deal time (you pick harder deals).
⇄
Prioritize Sales Cycle Speed
Pursue a wider ICP, prioritize fast-moving deals, push for quick decisions. Risk: more competitive deals where the buyer is evaluating 5 vendors; more "no decisions" from buyers not fully convinced. Result: lower win rate, shorter average cycle, needs more pipeline.

Most CROs try to optimize both simultaneously, which is usually a mistake. Pick the primary lever based on where you are in the growth cycle. Early stage (finding PMF): prioritize win rate — learn what makes buyers say yes. Growth stage (scaling): optimize for both by tightening ICP rather than loosening qualification standards.

Win Rate as an ICP Signal

Win rate is one of the most powerful validators of ICP accuracy. A systematic win rate analysis answers: "Are the accounts we think are our ICP actually the ones we win?" The results frequently surprise leadership teams.

Run this analysis annually: compare win rate across the ICP dimensions you believe are most important (industry, company size, tech stack, buyer persona). Often you'll find that win rate is 40%+ in your claimed top vertical but only 12% in the second-priority vertical — yet headcount and marketing spend are equally distributed between them. This is a misallocation of resources, and win rate data surfaces it.

The Win Rate ICP Test

If win rate is consistently below 15% in a segment for 3+ quarters, either your ICP definition is wrong (these companies aren't actually good fits) or your product-market fit in that segment is insufficient to support a competitive sales motion. In either case, reallocation of resources to higher-win-rate segments produces better revenue outcomes than continuing to invest in the low-win-rate segment.

Diagnosing a Win Rate Decline

Observed PatternLikely Root CauseDiagnostic TestFix
Win rate drops uniformly across all reps and segments Market or competitive change — competitor improved product/pricing, or category is commoditizing Win/loss interview with recent losses. What did the competitor show that changed the evaluation? Update battle cards, competitive positioning, and proof points. May need pricing adjustment.
Win rate drops for newer reps, stable for veterans Ramp problem — new reps not reaching proficiency before being given full territory Compare win rate by rep tenure. If win rate at months 1-4 is <10% but recovers to 25%+ by month 8, the issue is ramp quality, not market. Extend ramp period; increase manager support in first 6 months; improve enablement.
Win rate drops in specific verticals or deal sizes ICP drift — pursuing accounts outside your sweet spot, possibly driven by SDR sourcing too broadly Analyze win rate by ICP dimension. Which company profile characteristics correlate with wins vs. losses? Tighten ICP criteria, update SDR outreach targeting, remove low-win-rate accounts from rep focus.
Win rate drops in competitive deals only Competitor has improved significantly; your battle cards and positioning are outdated Win/loss analysis: what reasons are cited in competitive losses? What did the competitor say about you? Update battle cards based on Gong CI and win/loss interviews. Retrain reps on new competitive positioning within 30 days.
Win rate drops gradually over 4-6 quarters Market saturation in ICP; most obvious buyers already converted; remaining pool is harder Analyze whether average deal time-in-funnel is increasing. If yes, you are pursuing harder deals with longer cycles. Expand ICP adjacently (new vertical, new segment, new geography). May need new product or feature expansion to address new segments.
