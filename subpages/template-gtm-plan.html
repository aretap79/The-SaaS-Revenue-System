Section 1 — Overview: The 4-Part Planning Process

Every annual GTM plan follows the same four-question sequence. Skip any step and the plan will be either unrealistic or underambitious.

Step 1
Where are you going?
→ Set Realistic Goals via Benchmarking
Use external benchmark data (see Section 2) to define what top-quartile and median performance looks like for your stage. Set targets that are ambitious but grounded.
Step 2
Where are you now?
→ Bottoms-Up Model → Baseline Plan
Build the Baseline Plan: what revenue will you generate if you do nothing different? Use the Bottoms-Up model with current headcount, quotas, ramp schedules, and historical conversion rates.
Step 3
What's the gap?
→ Goal minus Baseline
The gap is not a problem — it is an opportunity. Quantify it precisely: How much additional ARR do you need beyond the current trajectory to hit your goal?
Step 4
How do we close it?
→ Target Plan with Levers
Identify the levers: rep productivity improvements, demand gen investment, headcount additions. Quantify the expected impact of each lever and build the Target Plan.
Contingency Plan — Required

Define your Contingency Plan before the year begins — not after you miss two quarters. The trigger for the Contingency Plan is either of: (1) runway drops below 12–18 months, or (2) you miss two consecutive quarters against the Baseline Plan. The Contingency Plan shows how you grow slower but reach breakeven — which channels to cut, which headcount additions to defer, and what the revised growth rate looks like.

Section 8 — Leading Indicator of Retention

The LIR belongs in the annual GTM plan because it is the primary signal that what you're selling is actually working. Include it explicitly.

LIR Definition for This Plan

LI of Retention: X% of customers do [specific action] every [time period]

Fill in this field before finalizing the plan. It is the single most important leading indicator of whether your retention assumptions will hold.

Cohort Chart Requirement

Produce a monthly cohort chart as part of the GTM plan. Present it at every board meeting alongside CCAC and LIUE data. See the LIR Journey Template for the full methodology and how to build the cohort chart.
