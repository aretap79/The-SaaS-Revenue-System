The 5 Channel Types

Every SaaS company uses a combination of go-to-market channels. The question is not which channel is best — it is which channel mix matches your ACV, buyer journey, and market coverage needs at each stage of growth.

Channel 1
Direct Sales

The default channel for B2B SaaS. Your own sales reps prospect, qualify, demo, negotiate, and close. You own the buyer relationship, you control the message, and you capture 100% of the revenue. The trade-off: it is the most expensive channel per dollar of revenue, and it scales linearly — each additional dollar of revenue requires more headcount.

Direct sales is optimal when ACV exceeds $25K (the economics support dedicated reps), when the buying decision is complex and requires relationship-building, and when you are still learning your ICP and need to control every touchpoint to extract market intelligence.

Best ACV Range
$25K – $500K+
CAC Multiple
1.0x (Baseline)
Margin
70–80% gross
Channel 2
VAR / Reseller Partners

Value-Added Resellers (VARs) and resellers buy your product at a discount (typically 15-35% for SMB VARs, 20-40% for enterprise integrators) and sell it to their own customer base with their own margin. They may add professional services, integration work, or complementary products. You access a customer segment or geography you could not economically cover with direct sales.

VARs work best when (1) the buyer already has a trusted advisory relationship with the reseller, (2) your product is a component of a larger solution the VAR builds, and (3) you cannot cost-effectively hire direct reps for the territory or segment.

Best ACV Range
$5K – $150K
CAC Multiple
0.4–0.7x
Margin
45–65% (net of discount)
Channel 3
Technology Alliance / ISV Partners

Technology alliances are integrations or co-sell agreements with complementary SaaS vendors targeting the same buyer. Salesforce ISV partners, AWS Marketplace listings, and HubSpot App Marketplace integrations are examples. You don't pay a resale discount — instead you share pipeline, co-market, and co-sell. The deal remains yours to close.

Alliance partnerships are the highest-leverage, lowest-cost channel when done right. A listing in the Salesforce AppExchange gives you access to 150,000+ Salesforce customers who have proven budget and willingness to buy add-on SaaS. The challenge: you are competing for mindshare among hundreds of ISVs. Partner managers and co-marketing investment are required to make marketplace presence convert.

Best ACV Range
$10K – $250K
CAC Multiple
0.5–0.8x
Margin
65–75% (minimal discount)
Channel 4
OEM / White-Label

OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) agreements embed your technology inside another company's product. You are invisible to the end customer — your partner brands and sells it. White-label is similar: your product is re-branded and sold by a larger company as their own offering. Revenue is typically a per-seat or revenue-share arrangement at 15-40% of the OEM partner's realized revenue.

OEM is a minority strategy — it works only when (1) your technology is deep infrastructure that complements a larger product, (2) direct access to the end customer is blocked or uneconomical, and (3) the volume of seats in the OEM partner's distribution justifies the lower per-unit economics. It is defensible only if switching costs are high; otherwise, the OEM partner will build the capability in-house once it's validated.

Best ACV Range
Low per-seat, high volume
CAC Multiple
0.1–0.3x
Margin
30–50% (deep discount)
Channel 5
Product-Led Growth (PLG)

PLG is a go-to-market strategy where the product itself is the primary driver of acquisition, activation, and expansion. Users discover the product through a free trial, freemium tier, or viral sharing mechanism; they experience value before speaking to a salesperson; and they upgrade or invite colleagues based on product experience alone. Slack, Dropbox, Figma, and Notion are canonical PLG examples.

PLG dramatically reduces CAC by compressing the top of funnel and eliminating (or reducing) sales-assisted discovery for small deals. The challenge: PLG requires a product that delivers immediate standalone value, a frictionless onboarding experience, and a data infrastructure that identifies expansion signals (Product-Led Sales, or PLS).

Best ACV Range
$0 – $25K (self-serve)
$25K+ (PLS motion)
CAC Multiple
0.1–0.4x (vs. direct)
Margin
75–85% (lower S&M burden)

Channel Decision Framework

Condition Recommended Channel Mix Rationale
ACV < $5K, high-volume self-serve PLG primary + inbound direct secondary Economics do not support direct reps; product must sell itself. CAC must be <$500 to achieve payback in 12 months on sub-$5K ACV.
ACV $5K–$25K, broad mid-market PLG + inside sales (PLS motion) + VAR for geographic coverage Reps close PLG-qualified accounts; partners fill geographic gaps without headcount investment.
ACV $25K–$100K, named accounts Direct enterprise + technology alliances + deal registration for VARs Complexity and relationship requirements demand direct reps; alliances multiply coverage without diluting gross margin.
ACV $100K+, strategic enterprise Direct enterprise (specialist reps) + GSI/consulting partnerships for implementation Global Systems Integrators (Accenture, Deloitte, PwC) carry significant enterprise credibility and create implementation stickiness that protects NRR.
New geographic market entry Distribution/reseller first, then direct overlay after year 2 Local partners de-risk market entry; use deal registration to protect partner economics while building direct capability.
Underserved SMB tail Marketplace listing (Salesforce/HubSpot AppExchange) + self-serve Leverage platform distribution to monetize a segment your direct team cannot economically cover.

Partner Program Design

Partner Tiering

Most mature partner programs use a 3-tier structure. Tiers define what the partner earns, what they're required to do, and what resources they receive. Tier thresholds are typically set so that fewer than 20% of partners reach the highest tier — scarcity drives partner investment.

Gold / Premier
  • $X ARR sourced per year
  • Dedicated partner manager
  • Joint business plan
  • 30-40% discount/margin
  • MDF access
  • Co-sell support from AEs
  • Early access to product roadmap
  • Named reference in marketing
Silver / Select
  • $Y ARR sourced per year
  • Pooled partner manager
  • Quarterly business review
  • 20-30% discount/margin
  • Limited MDF access
  • Self-service co-sell tools
  • Access to partner portal
Registered
  • Baseline certification required
  • Deal registration only
  • 15-20% discount/margin
  • No MDF
  • Self-serve resources only
  • Portal access
Deal Registration

Deal registration is the mechanism by which a partner claims ownership of an opportunity, triggering a protected discount for that deal. Without deal registration, partners fear your direct reps will poach their leads and undercut them on price. With deal registration, partners can confidently invest in an opportunity knowing their margin is protected.

Deal registration rules must be explicit: (1) registration window — how long after registration is the deal protected (typically 90-180 days)? (2) activity requirement — what does the partner have to do to keep the registration active? (3) conflict resolution — what happens if a direct rep is already in the account?

Market Development Funds (MDF)

MDF is co-marketing budget you allocate to partners based on tier, performance, and strategic priority. The market standard is 2-5% of partner-sourced revenue from the prior period, paid as a marketing fund for the next period. MDF is only effective when:

  • Partners are required to submit a pre-approved marketing plan before funds are released
  • Proof of execution (event attendance, ad spend receipts, lead lists) is required for reimbursement
  • Lead data from MDF activities is shared back to you for pipeline tracking
  • You have a Partner Marketing Manager reviewing plan quality before approval

MDF without accountability is a loss. Many companies eliminate MDF entirely after discovering that partners use it for executive dinners and branded merchandise with no pipeline impact.

The Channel Conflict Problem

Channel conflict occurs when your direct sales team and a reseller partner are competing for the same opportunity. It is the single biggest barrier to partner program health. Partners who experience channel conflict — where your direct rep undercuts their deal or ignores their registration — will stop investing in you.

Conflict Type Cause Prevention Mechanism
Direct rep poaches registered partner deal Rep quota pressure; partner not clearly tagged in CRM CRM integration with deal registration portal; rep quota credit only if partner is NOT registered on account
Partner and direct rep both call same account No named account list or territory exclusivity Named account tiers: "direct-only" accounts excluded from partner deal registration eligibility
Partner undercuts direct pricing Tiered discounts allow partners to win on price alone Minimum price floors enforced contractually; partner discount expressed as fixed rebate, not as unlimited price flexibility
Multiple partners claim same deal No first-registration rule First-registered timestamp wins; both partners notified; escalation path for disputes
Rule of Thumb

If your top direct enterprise AEs are losing deals to your own partners, your rules of engagement are broken. Partners should be additive coverage for segments and geographies your direct team doesn't prioritize — not competitors for the same whale accounts.

When NOT to Invest in Channel

Channel programs have significant ongoing costs: partner managers (1 PM per 15-20 active partners), partner portal technology, MDF, enablement materials, legal (partner agreements), and management attention. These fixed costs require a critical mass of partner-sourced revenue to justify.

  • Pre-product-market fit: If you haven't proven the direct sales motion, partners cannot follow a playbook that doesn't exist yet. Partners need a clear ICP, a repeatable demo, and reference customers to lean on.
  • Sub-$5M ARR: At early stage, channel economics rarely make sense — the overhead is disproportionate and you need to own every customer interaction to learn.
  • ACV below $2,500: The math doesn't work. A 25% discount on a $2K ACV is $500 margin to the partner — not enough to sustain a meaningful sales motion on their side.
  • Before your core message is locked: Partners can't position what you haven't figured out how to position. Every partner call becomes a confusing hand-wave. Get clear on your value prop first.

PLG Deep-Dive: The Product-Led Motion

Product-led growth is not just a pricing model — it is a fundamentally different GTM philosophy. In PLG, the product is the sales rep. Every UX decision, every onboarding flow, every notification is designed to move a user from awareness to value to expansion. Slack grew from 0 to $7B enterprise value in 4 years primarily through PLG.

The PLS (Product-Led Sales) Motion

PLS is the bridge between PLG and traditional enterprise sales. When a product-qualified account (PQA) reaches a usage threshold that signals enterprise intent — e.g., 10+ seats on a free plan, a specific power feature used, or a company-wide domain signup — a PLS rep reaches out to convert the engaged team into a paid enterprise contract.

The PLS motion is 3-5x more efficient than cold outbound because (1) the prospect already knows and likes the product, (2) there are real usage data to reference in the outreach, and (3) the champion is already identified — it's the person who invited the team.

PQA Definition — Product-Qualified Account
PQA Score = (Seat Count × 3) + (Feature Depth Score × 2) + (Recency Score × 1)
Seat Count: # of active users in the free plan. Feature Depth: # of high-value features used. Recency: days since last login (inverted). Set PQA threshold at e.g. score ≥ 15 → trigger PLS outreach.

Measuring Channel Effectiveness

Key Distinction

Partner-sourced pipeline: The partner found the lead — they own the initial relationship and brought the opportunity to you. Credit goes to the partner channel.
Partner-influenced pipeline: Your direct team was already working the account, but partner co-sell or partner reference accelerated or expanded the deal. The partner gets influence credit, not source credit.
