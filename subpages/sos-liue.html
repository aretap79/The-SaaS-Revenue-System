Why LIUE Exists
Unit economics — specifically LTV/CAC — are the definitive test of whether a GTM model is sustainable. The problem is that they require 12–24 months of customer cohort data to calculate meaningfully. LIUE solves this problem the same way LIR solved the retention problem.
Traditional LTV/CAC — The Problem
Definitive but delayed. To calculate LTV accurately, you need customers who have been with you long enough that their lifetime value is observable. That means 12–24 months minimum. By the time you know whether your GTM model works, you've already hired a team, burned through a significant portion of runway, and built a revenue plan around an unvalidated assumption.
LIUE — The Solution
Uses current, monthly data points that are the mathematical components of LTV/CAC. Not a proxy — these are the exact same variables inside the formula. Calculating LIUE today gives you the same signal as calculating LTV/CAC will give you 12–18 months from now, but in real time. Allows hiring decisions on a quarterly basis instead of waiting for cohort maturity.
Named in Parallel with LIR

LIUE and LIR are both "leading indicators" — they apply the same logic to different problems. LIR takes the lagging outcome "customer renewal" and decomposes it into an early product behavior. LIUE takes the lagging outcome "LTV/CAC > 3" and decomposes it into monthly business metrics. Both allow faster decision-making at critical stages.

Note: 'LIUE' (Leading Indicator of Unit Economics) is editorial shorthand used in this manual for the LTV/CAC decomposition methodology. LTV/CAC decomposition into trackable components is standard SaaS finance practice; the specific acronym is this manual's framing.

The Practical Decision Rule

If LIUE > 3 today: safe to add headcount. The current economics, if they hold, will produce sustainable unit economics at cohort maturity.
If LIUE < 3 today: identify which lever is broken, fix it, and don't add headcount until LIUE exceeds 3. Adding headcount when LIUE < 3 amplifies the problem — more headcount produces more customers with unsustainable unit economics.

The Full LIUE Formula
LIUE is derived by taking the LTV/CAC formula and substituting each term with its underlying components until every variable is one that can be measured from current data — not historical cohort maturity.
The Goal
LTV / CAC > 3
↓ expand LTV
LTV Formula
LTV = ACV × GM% ÷ Annual Churn Rate
↓ expand CAC into its two components
CAC — Marketing Component
CACMarketing = Cost per SQL ÷ SQL-to-Customer%
CAC — Sales Component
CACSales = Salesperson Monthly Cost ÷ (SQLs per Salesperson per Month × SQL-to-Customer%)
↓ combine into the full LIUE expression
The Full LIUE Formula
LIUE = LTV / CAC
LTV Numerator
ACV × GM% ÷ Annual Churn Rate
CAC Denominator
[Cost per SQL ÷ SQL-to-Customer%]  +  [Salesperson Monthly Cost ÷ (SQLs per Salesperson per Month × SQL-to-Customer%)]
LIUE > 3 → GTM Fit achieved. Safe to add headcount.

VariableFull NameTypical RangeHow to Calculate
ACV Annual Contract Value Varies by market Average signed contract value, annualized. Use median, not mean (avoid outlier distortion).
GM% Gross Margin % 70–85% for SaaS (Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue. Include all COGS: hosting, support, implementation.
Annual Churn Rate Annual Revenue Churn <10% target; <5% excellent ARR churned in 12 months ÷ beginning ARR. Can estimate as monthly churn × 12 for early cohorts.
Cost per SQL Marketing cost per Sales-Qualified Lead Varies by channel and ACV Total marketing spend attributed to SQL generation ÷ SQLs produced in that period.
SQL-to-Customer% Win Rate from SQL Stage 20–40% typical for B2B SaaS Closed-won deals ÷ total SQLs from same period. Use 3–6 month aged data to capture full close cycle.
Salesperson Monthly Cost Fully loaded rep cost per month $15–25K/month all-in Base salary ÷ 12 + variable OTE ÷ 12 + benefits + tools + management overhead. Use 115–125% of total cash comp as fully loaded multiplier.
SQLs per Salesperson per Month Rep productivity metric 4–10 depending on ACV/segment Pull from CRM: SQLs assigned to rep in a given month. Use ramped reps only (exclude first 90 days).
The LIUE Threshold: Why > 3?
The 3× threshold is a widely-cited rule of thumb in SaaS unit economics (originating from David Skok at Matrix Partners) — treat it as a useful heuristic rather than a precise standard. Understanding the math behind it — and the meaning of different LIUE levels — prevents both premature scaling and unnecessary conservatism.
What the Thresholds Mean

A LIUE of 3 means: for every dollar spent acquiring a customer, you expect to generate $3 in gross profit over the customer's lifetime. This provides enough margin to cover operating expenses, reinvest in product, and still generate a return.

LIUEInterpretationImplicationAction
< 1.0 Destroying Value You spend more acquiring a customer than you'll ever earn back. Every customer is a net loss even before operating expenses. Stop all hiring immediately. Diagnose whether to fix CAC, fix churn, or exit the segment.
1.0–2.0 Marginal You recover CAC, but the margin is not sufficient to justify growth capital. Would need very long customer lifetimes and no overhead to be viable. No headcount growth. Significant improvement required before any scaling consideration.
2.0–3.0 Below Threshold Unit economics exist but are not yet strong enough for confident scaling. CAC payback is too long relative to the risk of churn before recovery. Optimize specific levers. Do not add headcount until consistently above 3.
3.0–5.0 GTM Fit Achieved Industry standard healthy range. Strong enough to scale with appropriate capital. Payback period is reasonable (typically 12–18 months). Scale at pace. Hire 2/month. Monitor LIUE monthly to ensure it holds under scale.
5.0+ Strong GTM Fit Excellent unit economics. High-growth SaaS companies consistently operate in this range. Every dollar of S&M investment is highly efficient. Accelerate hiring. Add demand gen channels. Begin aggressive market share capture.
Why Not Just Use Payback Period?

Payback period measures how long to recover CAC — useful, but incomplete. A company with 12-month payback and 3% annual churn has very different economics than one with 12-month payback and 30% churn. LTV/CAC (LIUE) captures both dimensions simultaneously: the payback speed AND the customer lifetime length that determines the total return. LIUE > 3 is the correct complete signal.

How to Improve LIUE
When LIUE < 3, the formula tells you exactly where the problem is. Each lever maps to a specific variable. Identify the weakest lever first — it will have the highest return on improvement effort.
LTV Levers — Increase the Numerator
Pull when: LTV is small relative to CAC — often caused by high churn or low ACV
Increase ACV Reprice, move upmarket, add seat/usage tiers. A 20% ACV increase raises LTV 20% directly. Example: $30K → $36K ACV improves LIUE by ~20% holding everything else constant.
Improve Gross Margin % Optimize infrastructure/hosting costs, reduce support burden through better onboarding, negotiate vendor contracts at scale. GM% 65% → 75% raises LTV by ~15%.
Reduce Annual Churn The highest leverage LTV lever. Annual churn 20% → 10% doubles LTV (10÷20 vs. 10÷10). LIR programs are the primary tool — churn is a product-market fit symptom, not a CS symptom.
Increase Contract Length Multi-year contracts reduce effective annual churn (customer can't leave at annual renewal) and improve LTV visibility. Offer 5–10% discount for 2-year, 10–15% for 3-year commitments.
CAC Levers — Reduce the Denominator
Pull when: CAC is high relative to LTV — often caused by poor conversion rates or low rep productivity
Reduce Cost per SQL Improve demand gen channel targeting. Shift spend to lower-cost channels. Test new content formats. Example: $1,000 → $750 cost per SQL reduces marketing CAC by 25%.
Increase SQL-to-Customer% Most powerful lever — appears in both CAC components. Improve discovery/qualification, tighten ICP definition, improve demo quality. 20% → 25% SQL-to-Customer% improves total CAC by ~20%.
Increase SQLs per Rep per Month Better demand gen alignment, improved prospecting cadence, better ICP targeting. 4 → 6 SQLs/rep/month reduces sales CAC by 33%. Most effective when combined with better demand gen.
Reduce Salesperson Monthly Cost Hire junior reps once playbook is fully codified. Use SDR + AE model (SDRs at lower cost per SQL; AEs at higher conversion). Don't reduce OTE below market — drives turnover.
The SQL-to-Customer% Leverage Effect

SQL-to-Customer% appears in the denominator of both CAC components simultaneously. This means a 10% improvement in SQL-to-Customer% (e.g., from 20% to 22%) reduces total CAC by approximately 9% in both components at once — a compounding improvement that no other single lever matches. Always improve this before anything else.

LIUE by Segment
Blended LIUE is a dangerous metric in growth-stage companies. It averages across segments with fundamentally different economics — masking both the segments that should be scaled and the segments that are destroying value.

A company's overall blended LIUE of 4 may contain an SMB segment with LIUE of 0.8 that is hidden inside an Enterprise segment with LIUE of 8. The blended number tells you nothing actionable. The segment-level number tells you to stop investing in SMB immediately and double down on Enterprise.

Calculate LIUE separately for each dimension:

  • Market segment — SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise, each independently
  • Demand gen channel — inbound, outbound, paid, partner, PLG — each independently
  • Product line — each product or bundle independently if multiple exist

LIUE Dashboard: How to Track It
LIUE is a monthly tracking metric, not a quarterly or annual one. Each of the four input metrics must be monitored every month so that degradation is caught before it compounds into a hiring decision problem.
The 5 Dashboard Metrics to Track Monthly
Dashboard Metric 1
LIUE Overall
The headline number. Must stay > 3. Track month-over-month trend — a declining trend is as important as the absolute level.
Dashboard Metric 2
LIUE by Segment
Separate calculation for each market segment and demand gen channel. Never rely on blended LIUE alone for scaling decisions.
Dashboard Metric 3
LTV Trend
Driven by ACV, GM%, and churn. Rising LTV trend = pricing power and retention improving. Declining LTV = churn or pricing problem developing.
Dashboard Metric 4
CAC Trend
Driven by Cost per SQL, SQL-to-Customer%, and rep productivity. Rising CAC is the most common failure mode in growth-stage companies. Catch it monthly, not quarterly.
Dashboard Metric 5
4 LIUE Input Metrics
SQLs per rep, Cost per SQL, SQL-to-Customer%, ACV — each tracked with a target line. Any metric below target for 2+ consecutive months triggers investigation.
LIUE Red Flags: Early Warning Signals
  • 🔴
    Rising Cost per SQL (2+ months). Demand gen efficiency is declining. Your best-performing channel is saturating, or targeting has drifted off-ICP. Investigate channel mix and campaign targeting before the CAC impact appears in LIUE.
  • 🔴
    Falling SQL-to-Customer% (on aged data). Win rate is declining. Often caused by: ICP drift (targeting less-qualified prospects), competitive pressure, or playbook degradation as new reps ramp. The most important lever — a small drop in win rate has a large LIUE impact.
  • 🔴
    Rising annual churn rate. LIR is breaking before LIUE shows it. Rising churn is almost always a symptom of PMF degradation — the wrong customers being acquired, or the product not delivering value consistently. Fix LIR first; churn follows LIR, not the reverse.
  • 🟡
    Declining ACV (average deal size). Reps are discounting to close or targeting smaller accounts. Both compress LTV. A 15% ACV reduction requires a 17% improvement elsewhere to hold LIUE constant. Track average ACV by rep and by month.
  • 🟡
    Declining SQLs per rep per month (on ramped reps). Rep productivity is falling. Causes: demand gen volume not keeping up with headcount, inbound channel saturating, reps not prospecting enough, leads being recycled. Requires immediate attention before it compounds across the whole team.
The Healthy LIUE Trend

A healthy Stage 2 company shows LIUE rising quarter-over-quarter as the playbook is refined. Month 1 LIUE of 2.5 → Month 6 LIUE of 3.8 → Month 12 LIUE of 5.1 is the expected arc. If LIUE is flat or declining despite active improvement work, something structural is wrong with the GTM motion or the product-market fit in that segment.

LIUE vs. Other Unit Economic Metrics
LIUE complements, but does not replace, other unit economic metrics. Each captures a different dimension. Understanding what each one measures — and what it misses — prevents over-reliance on any single signal.
MetricMeasuresData LagPredictivenessKey Limitation
LIUE (LTV/CAC Real-Time) Real-time unit economics using current monthly data Current data Very High — same formula as LTV/CAC, no lag Relies on churn estimate, which may be imprecise in early cohorts
LTV/CAC (Traditional) Historical unit economics from fully matured cohorts 12–24 month lag High — definitive signal from actual retention data Too slow for real-time scaling decisions; always describes the past
CAC Payback Period Months to recover acquisition cost from gross margin Current data Medium — doesn't account for customer lifetime after payback Two companies with same payback but different churn have very different economics
Magic Number S&M efficiency: new ARR ÷ prior-quarter S&M spend Quarterly lag Medium — good capital efficiency signal Doesn't differentiate between new customer acquisition and expansion ARR
CAC Ratio CAC as % of ACV (payback expressed as ratio of first year) Current data Medium — simple, directionally useful Ignores gross margin and churn rate — two companies with same CAC ratio can have very different LIUE
NRR (Net Revenue Retention) Revenue retained + expanded from existing customers Quarterly High — strong long-term retention and expansion proxy Doesn't measure new customer acquisition efficiency; can mask churn with expansion
Use All of These — But Start with LIUE

For GTM scaling decisions in Stage 2, LIUE is the primary metric. For board reporting and investor communication, add Magic Number and CAC Payback. For long-term health monitoring, add NRR. For final validation of GTM model before Series B, confirm with traditional LTV/CAC using matured cohorts. Each metric adds a piece; none is complete alone.
