1. CS Philosophy: LIR is the North Star
Customer Success is not a support function. It is a revenue function — responsible for retention, expansion, and the proof that your GTM motion is creating real value. Every CS activity should be traceable back to one outcome: LIR achievement.
⭐
The North Star: LIR Achievement
LIR (Leading Indicator of Retention) is the single most important early signal that a customer will retain and expand. When a customer achieves LIR, they have experienced the core value of your product — the thing that makes them stick. CS mission: ensure that every customer who is capable of achieving LIR does so, within time T. Everything else is supporting activity.
  • LIR achievement = the single leading indicator of retention = the validation of your entire GTM motion
  • Everything the CS team does — kickoffs, training, check-ins, QBRs — should be oriented around accelerating time to LIR
  • CS primary mission: ensure every capable customer achieves LIR in time T
  • CS secondary mission: expand revenue within retained accounts (drive NRR > 100%)
  • A customer who achieves LIR is 3–5x more likely to renew and 2x more likely to expand — LIR is not just a metric, it is the revenue foundation
LIR and GTM Alignment: Sales qualification, discovery, and CS onboarding must all reference the same LIR definition. Sales closes the customer on LIR achievement as the value anchor. CS delivers on that promise. Misalignment between what Sales promises and what CS delivers is the root cause of most onboarding friction and early churn.
2. The CS Onboarding Playbook (0 to LIR)
The onboarding journey is not a feature tour or a training curriculum — it is a structured path from contract signature to LIR achievement. Every phase has a specific goal, and every goal is a milestone on the path to confirmed customer value.
  • D0
    Phase 1
    Kickoff — Day 0 to Day 3
    Make the customer feel confident, set LIR as the explicit and shared success target, introduce the CSM as the primary contact, and create a shared implementation plan with LIR as the primary milestone.
    • Attendees: CSM + customer champion + key stakeholders who will use the product
    • Deliverable 1: Implementation timeline with LIR achievement date as the primary milestone — not feature rollout date
    • Deliverable 2: Shared success plan document — what does LIR look like for this customer specifically? How will we know it was achieved?
    • CSM Action: Review deal notes from AE, confirm LIR definition matches what was promised in the sale, flag any expectation gaps before they become issues
  • D4+
    Phase 2
    Onboarding — Day 4 to Day T/2
    Guided setup, structured training, and first value delivery. The goal is to reach the LIR precursor event — the first signal that the customer is on the path to LIR — before the midpoint assessment.
    • Guided Setup: 2–3 structured sessions to complete core configuration. CSM drives the agenda — do not wait for the customer to ask what to do next.
    • Training: End-user training for the primary use case that maps directly to LIR achievement
    • Quick Win: Identify and celebrate the first LIR precursor event — the customer's first experience of the specific value your product delivers
    • Check-In Cadence: Weekly during onboarding. Standing agenda: what was accomplished, what's blocking, what's next before LIR
    • Risk Signal: If customer misses 2 consecutive weekly check-ins, flag as Yellow. If they miss 3, flag as Red.
  • T/2
    Phase 3
    Mid-Point Assessment — Day T/2
    A formal assessment of whether the customer is on track to achieve LIR by Day T. This is not a check-in — it is a diagnostic gate.
    • On Track: Customer has achieved the LIR precursor event. CSM confirms milestones and accelerates toward final LIR event.
    • Off Track: LIR precursor has not been achieved. Trigger the LIR Intervention Protocol immediately (see Section 3). Do not wait until Day T.
    • Review: What blockers exist? Are they technical (product), organizational (customer hasn't allocated resources), or motivational (champion has deprioritized)?
  • Day T
    Phase 4
    LIR Achievement — Day T
    The LIR event has been confirmed as achieved. This is the most important milestone in the customer lifecycle.
    • Confirmation: LIR must be instrumentable — confirmed via product analytics, not self-reported by the customer. "We think we're using it well" is not LIR achievement.
    • Sales Commission: Second 50% of AE commission is released upon confirmed LIR achievement — this aligns Sales incentives with actual customer value delivery
    • Documentation: Record the specific customer actions that led to LIR. What barriers were overcome? What accelerated progress? This data informs the onboarding playbook improvements.
    • Transition: Formal shift from "onboarding" cadence to "ongoing success" cadence. Relationship deepens — from project-mode to partner-mode.
LIR Achieved
The Onboarding Finish Line
When LIR is confirmed, celebrate it explicitly with the customer. Acknowledge the milestone in writing, thank the champion, and use this moment to plant the seed for the expansion conversation: "Now that you've proven this works for your team — let's talk about where you want to go from here."
3. The LIR Intervention Protocol
When a customer is at risk of missing LIR, the intervention protocol is triggered immediately — not at the end of the onboarding period. Early intervention at T/2 has 3x the success rate of intervention at T. Late action on churn risk is the most expensive mistake in CS.
Trigger: Customer has not achieved the LIR precursor event by Day T/2, or on-track signals are weak (no engagement, missed check-ins, no product usage in the past 7 days).
  • Diagnostic Call 30-minute session with the customer champion to understand blockers. Is the issue technical (product isn't working as expected), organizational (resources haven't been allocated), or prioritization (other things have taken over)? The diagnosis determines the response.
  • Escalation to Economic Buyer If the blocker is organizational — customer hasn't allocated internal resources, or champion has lost organizational support — loop in the Economic Buyer. This is a structured escalation, not a complaint call. Frame it as: "I want to make sure we're set up for you to get full value from this investment."
  • Recovery Plan A 30-day remediation plan with specific milestones and weekly checkpoints. The plan should be written and signed off by both the CSM and the customer champion. Shared accountability is critical — a verbal agreement is not a recovery plan.
  • Executive Alignment If the recovery plan is rejected, ignored after 2 weeks, or the customer fails to meet 2 consecutive recovery milestones, flag as high-churn risk. Escalate to VP CS. Prepare a save plan that addresses the root cause — not just the symptoms.

Escalation Matrix

Risk Level Trigger Action Owner
Green On-track at T/2. Precursor event achieved. Standard cadence. Accelerate toward LIR confirmation. CSM
Yellow Off-track at T/2. No precursor event. Low engagement signals. Diagnostic call within 48 hours. Build recovery plan. Flag to manager. CSM + CS Manager
Red Missed LIR at Day T. Recovery plan not adopted or failing. Executive escalation. VP CS joins next customer call. AE looped in to support champion. VP CS + AE
Critical No meaningful engagement after T+30. No product usage. Champion unresponsive. Churn prevention protocol: CRO direct outreach to Economic Buyer. Save plan prepared. CRO + Economic Buyer call
4. Onboarding Swim Lanes
Not all customers follow the same onboarding path. The appropriate level of CSM involvement is determined by segment, complexity, and strategic value — not by default. Over-investing in SMB onboarding destroys unit economics. Under-investing in enterprise onboarding destroys retention.
Swim Lane 1
Self-Serve / SMB
LIR Timeline 14–30 days
CSM Time <2 hrs / month
Escalation Path Pooled CSM team
Key Activities
  • Product-led onboarding with in-app checklists and tooltips
  • Automated email nurture tied to LIR precursor milestones
  • LIR tracked via product analytics — no self-reporting
  • CSM intervenes only on health score drop below threshold
  • Office hours for questions — not dedicated calls
Swim Lane 2
Mid-Market, Guided
LIR Timeline 30–60 days
CSM Time 3–5 hrs / month
Escalation Path Named CSM + Manager
Key Activities
  • Weekly 30-min CSM calls during onboarding
  • Structured milestone checklist with shared visibility
  • Shared Slack or Teams channel for async questions
  • Mid-point formal assessment at T/2
  • Champion empowerment kit — ROI data, internal sell materials
Swim Lane 3
Enterprise, White-Glove
LIR Timeline 60–120 days
CSM Time 8–15 hrs / month
Escalation Path Named CSM + VP CS
Key Activities
  • Dedicated CSM with executive sponsor engagement from Day 0
  • Custom implementation plan — not templated
  • Formal QBRs from Month 3 onward
  • Weekly steering committee calls during initial onboarding
  • Integration support, security review support, change management assistance
5. Ongoing Success Motions (Post-LIR)
Once LIR is achieved, the CS relationship shifts from onboarding to partnership. The primary motions are QBRs (to maintain and expand ROI) and health score management (to identify risk before it becomes churn).

QBR (Quarterly Business Review) Framework

  • LIR Achievement Recap — Review the journey from Day 0 to LIR. Quantify the value delivered in the customer's own metrics. This anchors the value conversation before any renewal or expansion discussion.
  • Usage & Adoption Metrics — Present current product engagement data. Are the right users using the right features? Are power users growing or shrinking? Is usage trending toward or away from expansion?
  • ROI Delivered — Translate usage into business impact. Not "you sent 1,000 reports" — but "based on your usage data, your team has saved approximately X hours and $Y in [cost category]." Use the metrics established in discovery as the frame.
  • Roadmap Alignment — Share 1–2 upcoming features most relevant to the customer's stated goals. This signals you're investing in their future, not just their past. Do not share the full roadmap — only what's directly relevant to their priorities.
  • Expansion Opportunity — If ROI has been demonstrated and the relationship is strong, open the expansion conversation. "Given the value you're seeing in [current area], we've been thinking about where you might get the most value next." Use this as discovery, not a pitch.
QBR Attendees: CSM + customer champion is the minimum. For expansion conversations, the economic buyer should attend if possible. "You may want to include [Economic Buyer name] in this one — I want to show them the ROI data we've been tracking." This naturally draws the buyer back in for expansion.

Signal Weight Green Yellow Red

6. Expansion Motion
The most efficient revenue in SaaS comes from existing customers who have already proven ROI. Expansion built on a foundation of LIR achievement and demonstrated value closes faster, discounts less, and churns at a fraction of the rate of new logo ARR.

Expansion Frameworks

  • Land and Expand: Start with one team or use case. Prove LIR and ROI. Expand to adjacent teams using the same champion, who is now a proven internal sponsor. Reduce customer acquisition friction by making expansion feel like an obvious next step, not a new sale.
  • Multi-Product: After LIR is confirmed on Product A, introduce Product B to the same champion with a direct link to their stated next priority. Use the ROI data from Product A as the proof point for Product B's value potential.
  • Upsell Triggers: Usage approaching plan limits, new hires added to the team, a strategic initiative aligned to a new product feature, or a company event (funding, acquisition, growth milestone) that creates new buying intent.

Expansion Conversation Timing

Earliest
LIR + 30 Days
Customer has tangible ROI to reference. Timing is aggressive — only appropriate if the champion proactively raises expansion interest. Do not push; respond.
Optimal
QBR at Month 6
Full quarter of post-LIR data. Relationship is established. Champion has had time to demonstrate internal success. Economic buyer has seen ROI in a formal review. Best conversion rate.
Red Flag
Before LIR
Selling more to a customer who hasn't yet proven value is the fastest way to lose them. They are in a fragile state. Expansion before LIR increases churn probability — never do this.
7. CS Metrics
These seven metrics tell the full CS story — from onboarding effectiveness to expansion engine health. Track monthly at the portfolio level and by CSM. Deviations from target at the CSM level reveal coaching opportunities.
Metric Definition Target
LIR Achievement Rate % of new customers achieving LIR by Day T ≥60–80% (match PMF threshold)
Time to LIR Days from contract start to confirmed LIR event — track monthly average Minimize — track trend, not absolute
Gross Revenue Retention (GRR) ARR retained from existing customers excluding expansion, only counting downsells and churn ≥90%
Net Revenue Retention (NRR) ARR retained from existing customers including expansion, minus churn and downsell ≥110% (≥120% for growth stage)
Health Score Composite score of LIR achievement, engagement, support, and renewal signals 80%+ of portfolio Green
CSAT / NPS Customer satisfaction (CSAT) and Net Promoter Score measured post-LIR and at QBR NPS >40, CSAT >85%
Expansion ARR New ARR generated from existing customers via upsell, cross-sell, and seat expansion >30% of new ARR target
8. Hiring for CS
CS hiring is different from sales hiring. The characteristics that drive retention and expansion — empathy, strategic thinking, cross-functional influence — are different from those that drive prospecting and closing. Use a structured scorecard to avoid hiring based on personality fit alone.

Four core criteria for CS hire evaluation. High-weight criteria are table-stakes — a miss on either is disqualifying.

High Weight — Table Stakes
Relationship Management
Builds genuine trust with customers at all levels — from end user to economic buyer. Examples of long-term customer partnerships where they were the primary reason for renewal and expansion. Not just "liked by customers" — trusted and relied upon as an advisor.
High Weight — Table Stakes
Strategic Thinking
Understands customer business goals beyond product usage. Proactively identifies risk and opportunity before they arise — not in response to customer escalations. Can translate product capabilities into customer business outcomes without being prompted.
Medium Weight
Cross-Functional Collaboration
Works effectively with Sales, Product, and Marketing. Known by other departments as a high-quality handoff partner — provides specific, actionable context rather than vague notes. Escalates issues constructively, with solutions rather than just problems.
Medium Weight
Growth Mindset
Learns from losses — can articulate specific things they've changed based on a customer churn they experienced. Actively seeks coaching rather than waiting for it. Demonstrates measurable improvement call-over-call and QBR-over-QBR.
Structured Scorecard: Use the CS Hire Interview Scorecard to score each candidate numerically on these four criteria. Gut-feel hiring in CS produces high variance in outcomes. Structured evaluation produces consistent CS teams. See template-scorecard-cs.html for the full scorecard.
