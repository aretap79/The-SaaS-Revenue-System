How to Use This Worksheet

This worksheet has two phases. Where you are in your product journey determines which phase to use — and many companies will be working on both simultaneously.

Phase 1 · Pre-PMF
LIR Hypothesis Testing
Test 2–3 LIR hypotheses in parallel using monthly cohort data. Identify which event (E), time window (T), and rate (P) correlates most strongly with 12-month retention.
Phase 2 · Post-PMF
LIR + LIUE Tracking
Lock in your confirmed LIR metric and track it monthly by cohort. Begin tracking LIUE quarterly. Use both metrics to determine GTM Fit and make hiring decisions.
Key Definitions

LIR (Leading Indicator of Retention): A customer behavior event (E) that, when completed within a defined time window (T) by a minimum percentage of new customers (P), predicts that those customers will renew at 12 months at a significantly higher rate than customers who don't complete it.

LIUE (Leading Indicator of Unit Economics): A composite metric that measures the relationship between customer lifetime value (LTV) and customer acquisition cost (CAC). LIUE > 3 for two consecutive quarters is the threshold for GTM Fit — the point at which it is safe to hire a first sales leader and begin scaling the sales motion.

Pre-PMF: LIR Hypothesis Options

Define 2–3 LIR hypotheses and test them in parallel. Each hypothesis specifies an Event (E), a Time Window (T), and a Target Rate (P). Track monthly cohorts for each option and compare each rate to 12-month retention outcomes.

How to Define a LIR Event (E)

The LIR event should be a specific, measurable customer behavior that represents meaningful product adoption — not just logging in. Examples: "created 3 or more reports," "invited 2 or more team members," "completed first integration," "processed first live transaction." The event should require the customer to get genuine value from the product, not just touch it once.

3-Month Cohort Tracking: LIR Correlation Analysis

Correlation Validation Rule

For the winning LIR option to be confirmed, the gap between LIR-hit and LIR-miss 12-month retention must be at least 20 percentage points across at least 3 consecutive cohorts. Example: customers who achieve LIR retain at 88%; customers who don't retain at 52%. Gap = 36pp ✓ Confirmed. If the gap is less than 20pp, the event you've chosen is not a strong enough predictor — you need to refine the event definition.

LIUE Component Tracker

Track LIUE quarterly. The formula requires 7 input variables. Enter quarterly actuals and compute LIUE. Trend over time is as important as the absolute value — you want to see LIUE improving quarter-over-quarter on the path to GTM Fit.

LIUE Formula Components

Input 1
ACV
Annual Contract Value — average revenue per new customer in the quarter
Input 2
GM%
Gross Margin % — product margin after direct COGS (exclude S&M)
Input 3
Churn
Annual Gross Revenue Churn Rate — % of ARR lost from existing customers per year
Input 4
Cost/SQL
All-in cost to generate one Sales Qualified Lead (marketing spend + SDR cost)
Input 5
SQL→Cust%
SQL-to-Customer conversion rate — % of SQLs that become paying customers
Input 6
RepCost/Mo
All-in monthly cost of one AE (salary + benefits + quota-based variable at 100% attainment)
Input 7
SQLs/Rep/Mo
Average SQLs generated per rep per month (inbound + outbound combined)
Output
LIUE
Computed from inputs above. Target: > 3 for GTM Fit signal.
LIUE Formula
LTV = ACV × GM% ÷ Churn
CAC = (Cost/SQL ÷ SQL-to-Cust%) + (RepCost/Mo ÷ SQLs/Rep/Mo)
LIUE = LTV ÷ CAC
GTM Fit threshold: LIUE > 3 for 2 consecutive quarters

LIUE > 3 for Two Consecutive Quarters = GTM Fit

GTM Fit is confirmed when LIUE exceeds 3 for two consecutive quarters. This is the signal that your unit economics are healthy enough to safely hire a first sales leader and begin scaling the GTM motion. Before this point, hiring additional sales headcount accelerates cash burn without proportional revenue return. After this point, it's a math problem: add headcount at the proven unit economics rate.

Interpretation Guide

Use this decision tree when your LIR or LIUE metrics are showing unexpected patterns. Each scenario points to a different root cause and a different intervention.

If Condition → LIR Rate < 60% for 2+ Consecutive Cohorts
Onboarding or ICP Problem — Act Immediately
Three possible root causes: (1) Your ICP definition has drifted — you're selling to customers who don't get value from the product. Audit the last 2 cohorts' firmographics vs. your ICP definition. (2) The LIR event is too hard to achieve — your onboarding process isn't driving customers to the event fast enough. Audit the onboarding journey step by step. (3) The LIR event itself was the wrong hypothesis — it doesn't correlate with value delivery for your evolved product. Run LIR hypothesis tests again with new event candidates.
If Condition → LIUE < 3 Despite High LIR Rate
Unit Economics Problem — Diagnose LTV vs. CAC Independently
High LIR + low LIUE means you have strong retention but poor efficiency. Diagnose which side of the equation is broken: If LTV is low (ACV × GM% ÷ Churn is small), the problem is pricing, gross margin structure, or still-too-high churn. If CAC is high (cost per SQL or rep productivity is poor), the problem is demand gen cost or rep ramp time and quota attainment. Fix the right side before scaling anything.
If Condition → LIR Rising but LIUE Falling
Selling Right Customers, But with Poor Efficiency
This pattern means your product is getting stickier (more customers achieving LIR), but your acquisition cost is increasing or rep productivity is declining. Check: (1) Is demand gen cost per SQL rising as you exhaust easy channels? (2) Is rep ramp time increasing as you hire less experienced reps? (3) Is SQL-to-close rate declining because ICP qualification is getting noisier? The efficiency side needs attention before headcount expansion.
If Condition → LIR Falling but LIUE Rising
Efficiency Improving but ICP Drift Underway — Churn Spike Incoming
This is the most dangerous pattern. Improving LIUE makes things look good, but falling LIR means the customers you're acquiring are less and less likely to get value from the product. Churn hasn't shown up yet because the cohorts haven't reached renewal. It will. Act immediately: (1) Audit the ICP of recent cohorts vs. historical LIR-hit cohorts. (2) Tighten qualification criteria even if it reduces new logo volume. (3) Add LIR achievement as a leading indicator on the board dashboard so this pattern is visible before churn materializes.
If Condition → LIR ≥ 65% AND LIUE > 3 for 2 Consecutive Quarters
GTM Fit Confirmed — Begin Scaling the GTM Motion
This is the signal you've been waiting for. You now have the evidence needed to: (1) Hire your first sales leader (use the First Sales Leader Interview Scorecard). (2) Begin structured rep hiring based on proven unit economics. (3) Set a formal revenue target for the next 12 months based on your LIUE model. (4) Build a formal LIR monitoring program into the CS onboarding process to protect the metrics that got you here.
