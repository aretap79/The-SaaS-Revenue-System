Paid Marketing Philosophy

Paid channels are dials — not foundations. They amplify what already works, but cannot substitute for product-market fit, ICP clarity, or message resonance. Getting these principles right prevents budget waste at scale.

01
Validate Before Scaling
Never scale paid spend until both cost per SQL and LIR achievement are validated in a test run. Scaling an unvalidated channel multiplies waste, not results.
02
Stage-Gated Investment
Stage 2: maximum 1–2 paid channels, tested and validated. Stage 3: diversified paid portfolio with strict LIUE tracking per channel. Never expand before the current channels are proven.
03
LIUE Is the Gate
Paid channels must maintain LIUE >3 to justify continued investment. Turn up channels with LIUE >5. Turn down channels with LIUE <3. Kill channels with LIUE <1.
Golden Rule

A paid channel that generates low-cost SQLs but with below-threshold LIR achievement is destroying LIUE — the cheap acquisition cost is being offset by poor retention and low LTV. Always validate both the numerator (LTV via LIR) and denominator (CAC) of LIUE before declaring a channel healthy.

LinkedIn Ads Best Practices

LinkedIn is the highest-precision B2B targeting platform but also the most expensive. These best practices are specific to B2B SaaS demand generation — not generic ad advice.

Audience Targeting

Job title + seniority + company size — mirror your ICP definition exactly. Do not use interest targeting or skill targeting for cold audiences; they introduce too much noise.

Minimum audience size: 50,000 to avoid creative fatigue too quickly. Maximum: 500,000 for cold audiences (precision matters more than reach).

Exclude competitors and current customers from cold audiences to protect efficiency.

Creative Strategy

Single image (1200×627px) outperforms video for B2B conversion in most tests. Video works for awareness; static works for conversion.

Dark backgrounds with white text perform consistently well. Avoid stock photography — people scroll past it. Use real data visualizations, real customer quotes, or abstract design.

A/B test 2 creative variants at all times. Replace the underperformer every 4 weeks.

Copy Framework

Lead with pain, not product. "Still managing [problem] manually?" outperforms "Introducing [Product Name]" by 3–5× on average for cold audiences.

First line must earn the scroll stop. Keep body copy under 150 characters before the "See More" fold. CTA text should match the offer exactly: "Download the guide" > "Learn More."

Offer Strategy

Free content for cold audiences (guide, report, checklist) converts significantly better than direct demo requests. Warm up first, then ask for time.

Frequency cap: 3–4 impressions per person per week. Anything above that triggers ad fatigue and deteriorates CTR.

Landing pages must be dedicated, have no navigation, and match the ad message exactly — "message match" is the #1 landing page conversion factor.

Budget Allocation Model

Paid marketing budget should be allocated quarterly using LIUE by channel as the primary reallocation signal. The model below is a starting framework for companies in Stage 2–3 with $5K–$50K monthly paid budgets.

Proven Channels (positive LIUE) 70%
1–2 channels with validated cost per SQL and LIR achievement. These channels get incremental scaling — increase budget 10–20% per quarter while LIUE holds above 3.
Experiments (new channels) 20%
Fixed budget with clear success metrics: target cost per SQL, target LIR rate, and a 60-day test window. Kill experiments that do not hit both metrics. Graduate experiments that do.
Brand Awareness (Stage 3 only) 10%
Not directly attributed to pipeline. Builds long-term demand. Only appropriate once Stage 2 channels are fully optimized and LIUE is healthy. Skip in Stage 2.

Quarterly Budget Review Process

  1. Pull LIUE by channel for the quarterCalculate LTV/CAC by channel using cohort-level data. Be conservative — use actual LIR achievement rates, not theoretical LTV.
  2. LIUE > 5: increase budget 25–50%These channels are generating outsized returns. Scale aggressively while the signal holds, but monitor for efficiency degradation as volume increases.
  3. LIUE 3–5: maintain budgetThese channels are healthy. Hold investment, optimize creatives and landing pages, and look for marginal efficiency improvements.
  4. LIUE 1–3: reduce budget and investigateSomething is wrong — either the cost per SQL has risen, LIR achievement has dropped, or both. Do not scale a channel that cannot clear LIUE 3.
  5. LIUE < 1: pause and redesignThe channel is consuming more CAC than it recovers in LTV. Pause immediately. Investigate whether the problem is targeting, offer, creative, or fundamental channel-ICP misalignment.
