What Is the Bottoms-Up Model?

A financial model built from GTM activity inputs — not top-down revenue targets handed down from a board deck.

Most companies build their revenue forecast the wrong way: starting with a number the board wants to see, then working backward to justify it. The bottoms-up model flips this. It starts with what your GTM team can actually produce — rep productivity, pipeline generation, headcount capacity, and retention — and builds the revenue forecast from those real inputs.

The result is a monthly forecast you can defend, a plan your team believes in, and a hiring roadmap that's tied to real output rather than optimism.

The Four Inputs

The bottoms-up model is built from four core inputs: (1) productivity per rep, (2) demand gen pipeline, (3) headcount plan, and (4) retention and expansion. These four levers, combined, produce your monthly revenue forecast with sensitivity analysis.

Use Case 1
Set Realistic Quotas
Anchor quota in rep productivity data — not what the CFO needs the rep to produce.
Use Case 2
Determine Hiring Pace
Know exactly when to add headcount based on pipeline coverage ratios and ramp timelines.
Use Case 3
Measure GTM Fit
Track whether your unit economics are improving or degrading quarter over quarter.
Use Case 4
Present to Board
Show baseline, target, and contingency plans built from the same underlying model.

The 4 Drivers (from Stage 2 Capital GTM Plan)

Each driver feeds a specific part of the model and produces a measurable output. Improve all four and you improve the forecast.
Driver What It Is Output Key Consideration
Rep Productivity What a rep needs to hit quota: average deal size + pipeline conversion rate Leading Indicators of Unit Economics Focus on improving productivity before adding headcount. More reps with broken unit economics just scales the problem.
Cost to Generate Qualified Pipeline Total S&M cost including headcount, marketing spend, tools, and overhead Demand Generation Plan Determine number of qualified opps needed by channel; align marketing initiatives to each channel's cost-per-SQL.

Customer Retention / Expansion The ability to forecast, retain, and expand existing customer revenue Leading Indicator of Retention Use the LIR framework; see Science of Scaling materials for detailed implementation instructions.

The Model Structure

The revenue model is a simple waterfall. Every line is either a source of ARR or a drain on it. The discipline is in making each line defensible.
▶ Starting ARR — ARR carried into the period from prior month
+ New ARR — ARR from new logos closed during the period
+ Expansion ARR — ARR from upsell and cross-sell of existing customers
− Contraction ARR — ARR lost from downgrades (customers paying less)
− Churn ARR — ARR lost from full cancellations
= Total ARR (End of Period) — Your closing ARR, which becomes next month's Starting ARR

Key Model Assumptions to Set

AE Productivity
New customers / AE / month
How many new logos can a fully-ramped AE close each month? Ground this in actual historical data, not aspirations.
AE Quota
Monthly quota by ramp stage
Set different quota levels for months 1–2, 3–4, and 5+ to reflect ramp reality. See Section 4.
CS Expansion
Expansion ARR / CSM / month
How much expansion ARR does each CSM drive monthly? Separate from new logo quota.
Gross Margin %
Revenue retained after COGS
Required for unit economics calculations. Target 70–80%+ for SaaS.
Pipeline Split
Inbound vs. outbound %
Example: 80% inbound / 20% outbound. Affects CAC and the cost-per-SQL calculation for each channel.
LIR Threshold
P% achieving E event in T days
The leading indicator of retention. Set the threshold that predicts long-term customer health.

The 25% Buffer Rule

Always add a 25% buffer to your salesperson cost to account for three hidden costs: (1) sales management overhead — every 6–8 AEs requires a manager who is largely non-quota-carrying; (2) ramp period cost — you're paying full salary before they produce full revenue; (3) attrition and replacement cost — average B2B SaaS AE tenure is 18–24 months; replacement searches cost time and fees. Plan for this upfront.

AE Quota Setting

Quota is both a planning input and a motivational contract. The Science of Scaling approach is to design quotas that are achievable by most well-supported reps — not aspirational by design.
Quota Multiple
Annual Quota = 4–5× OTE
On-Target Earnings. This ratio ensures the comp plan is economically sustainable while remaining competitive for talent.
Monthly Quota
Annual Quota ÷ 12
Simple monthly division of annual quota. Adjust for seasonality if deal timing is heavily weighted to certain months.
Attainment SignalWhat It MeansCorrect Action
60–70% of reps at or above quota Healthy plan — quota is challenging but achievable Maintain design. Monitor for drift.
>80% of reps hitting quota Quotas are too low — you're leaving revenue and growth on the table Raise quotas next planning cycle. Do not adjust mid-year.
<50% of reps hitting quota Quotas too high OR reps were hired before pipeline was ready to support them Diagnose pipeline coverage first. Then revisit quota or hiring timing.

How to Use the Bottoms-Up Model in Board Meetings

The bottoms-up model is most powerful when presented alongside your leading indicators — not as a standalone financial table. Structure the board narrative in this order.
1
Start with the LIR Cohort Chart
Show Product-Market Fit health. Are customers achieving the leading indicator of retention at the expected rate? This establishes confidence that the product deserves to be scaled.
2
Show the LIUE Dashboard
Present Leading Indicators of Unit Economics — GTM Fit health. Are your CAC, pipeline conversion, and AE productivity trending in the right direction?
3
Present the Bottoms-Up Model (Baseline Plan)
Show the model built from current rep productivity, pipeline, and headcount. This is the plan you will execute if everything goes to plan.
4
Present the Target Plan
Show the stretch plan with specific productivity improvement and pipeline growth assumptions. Be explicit about what has to be true for the target plan to materialize.
5
Present the Contingency Plan
What happens if you miss two quarters or runway drops below 12–18 months? Show the pre-built response: which hires get deferred, which programs get paused, and what the revised model looks like. Boards fund leaders who plan for failure, not just success.
The Credibility Rule

A board presentation built from a bottoms-up model — even if the numbers are lower than a top-down wish list — creates more investor confidence than inflated projections that don't survive scrutiny. Show your work. A CFO or board member who understands your unit economics will ask fewer alarming questions because they'll understand where the number comes from.
