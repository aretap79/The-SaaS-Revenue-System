Demand Generation Channel Guide

Demand gen is the engine that feeds your sales pipeline. In Stage 2, you need one scalable, measurable channel. In Stage 3, you diversify across multiple aligned channels. Every channel must pass the same four criteria.

The 4 Channel Selection Criteria

Every demand gen channel must meet ALL four criteria before investing meaningful budget or headcount. A channel that fails even one criterion will waste resources and generate misleading pipeline signals.

Criterion 1
Alignment
Are your ICP buyers active and reachable on this channel? A channel with perfect targeting tools is worthless if your buyers aren't present. Validate that your ICP spends time and attention in this channel before investing.
Criterion 2
Measurability
Can you track exact cost per SQL from this channel — not just cost per lead or cost per click? Pipeline attribution must be traceable back to the channel that generated each SQL.
Criterion 3
Rapid Testability
Can you run a 30–60 day test to validate both cost per SQL and pipeline volume? Channels with 12-month feedback loops (pure SEO, brand campaigns) are Stage 3 plays, not Stage 2 test candidates.
Criterion 4
Customer Success Alignment
Do leads from this channel achieve LIR at above-threshold rates? A channel that generates cheap SQLs with low LIR achievement is destroying LIUE (LTV/CAC), not building it. Quality > quantity — always.
Key Insight — Quality Over Volume

A channel that generates cheap SQLs but with low LIR achievement is destroying LIUE, not building it. Before scaling any channel, verify that its customers achieve LIR at or above the company-wide threshold. Drop channels that consistently underperform on LIR regardless of their cost per SQL.

Reading the Matrix

LIR Risk reflects how often leads from this channel fail to achieve the LIR event post-close. "Very Low" means channel leads reliably become retained customers. Deep-Dive: Outbound (Cold Email + LinkedIn)

Outbound is the go-to Stage 2 channel for B2B SaaS companies with a defined ICP and a compelling value proposition. Fast feedback loop, directly measurable, and controllable at any volume.

📤
Cold Email + LinkedIn Outbound
Best for: defined ICP, compelling value proposition, reps with strong discovery skills

Setup Guide

  1. Build your ICP listUse LinkedIn Sales Navigator or ZoomInfo to pull companies that match your ICP firmographic and technographic criteria. Target 500–2,000 accounts per rep cycle.
  2. Enrich with contact dataLayer in decision-maker contact data using Apollo.io or ZoomInfo. Prioritize verified emails over inferred emails. Target 2–3 contacts per account (economic buyer + champion).
  3. Build a 6–8 touch sequenceMix email and LinkedIn touches over 3–4 weeks. Lead with value — share a relevant insight, case study, or stat before pitching. Never open with a product pitch.
  4. Launch and measureTrack open rate, reply rate, and meeting booked rate by sequence. A/B test subject lines and opening lines. Optimize the top-performing variant.

Key Success Factors

  • Hyper-personalization — not mail merge. Reference the prospect's company, role, recent news, or specific pain point. Generic sequences get deleted.
  • Clear value statement. In the first sentence, the prospect should understand exactly what problem you solve and for whom. Ambiguity kills outbound.
  • Fast response time. Respond to inbound replies within 4 hours. Speed-to-reply rate is the #1 predictor of outbound meeting show rates.
  • Volume discipline. 50–100 new prospects per rep per week — quality-limited, not volume-limited. More personalization beats more volume every time.

Stage 2 Recommendation

Outbound is the highest-control Stage 2 channel. You control who you reach, when, and at what volume. Start with outbound to validate your ICP and value proposition before investing in channels with longer feedback loops.

Deep-Dive: Content & SEO

Content marketing builds compounding pipeline over time. Unlike paid channels, organic search traffic compounds — a post that drives 10 SQLs per month in Year 1 will drive 30 in Year 3 with proper authority building.

✍️
Content Marketing & SEO
Best for: companies with 12+ month horizons, strong thought leadership, and organic search demand in their category

Content Strategy by Stage

Stage 2 Focus
  • 1 primary content format — build authority in one category before diversifying
  • Long-form SEO articles (1,500–3,000 words) targeting problem-aware keywords
  • Customer case studies — proof that your product delivers LIR for the ICP
  • Minimum 2× per week publishing cadence to build SEO authority
Stage 3 Expansion
  • Thought leadership and original research reports
  • Video content and webinar series
  • Content clusters around 5–8 core ICP topics
  • Partner-authored and guest content for audience growth

ICP Content Alignment

Every piece of content should answer a question your ICP buyers are actively searching for. Map your content calendar to buyer journey stages — awareness (problem recognition), consideration (solution evaluation), and decision (vendor comparison).

Distribution Workflow

  1. PublishPost to your blog/website with proper SEO structure (H1, meta description, schema markup, internal links).
  2. ShareLinkedIn company page + employee advocacy program. Top-performing content gets a personal post from the CEO or VP Sales.
  3. EmailInclude in newsletter segment or relevant nurture sequence. High-value content becomes a lead magnet with gating.
  4. RepurposeSplit into 3–5 LinkedIn posts, 1–2 short videos, an infographic. One long-form piece should generate 10+ derivative pieces.
  5. SyndicateGuest post excerpts on industry publications, relevant Reddit communities, and Slack communities where your ICP is active.

Key Metric — Pipeline Influence

The Right Content Metric

Track % of SQLs that cite content as an influence — not just organic traffic volume. Organic traffic without pipeline influence is brand awareness, not demand gen. The metric that matters: Content-Influenced Pipeline as % of total pipeline.

Deep-Dive: Paid Marketing (SEM + Social)

Paid channels offer fast feedback and precise targeting, but require disciplined budget allocation and strict LIUE tracking. Never scale paid before proving cost per SQL and LIR achievement are both validated.

💰
Paid Search & Paid Social
Best for: companies with validated ICP, proven landing pages, and budget to test at meaningful scale ($5K+/month)

Brand

SEM Best Practices

  • Target non-branded keywords tied to ICP pain — "how to manage [problem]" beats branded terms for pipeline quality
  • Use high-intent search terms: competitor names, category + "software," "[pain point] + solution"
  • Landing page A/B testing is mandatory — test headline, CTA, and social proof combinations
  • Negative keyword lists: exclude irrelevant industries, job seeker terms, and non-ICP queries

LinkedIn Ads Best Practices

  • Audience: job title + seniority + company size — mirror your ICP definition exactly
  • Offer content downloads (guides, reports) for cold audiences — not product demos
  • Frequency cap: 3–4 impressions per person per week to avoid ad fatigue
  • Retargeting: pixel all website visitors and build retargeting audiences after 200+ page views

Deep-Dive: Events & Webinars

Events generate high-quality, high-intent pipeline but with longer lead times and higher cost per SQL. Best for Stage 3 when you have a defined channel strategy and can invest in execution quality.

🎤
Webinars, Field Events & Conferences
Best for: companies with strong brand, SME thought leaders, and highly concentrated ICP at specific events

Webinars

  • Host 1–2 per month on ICP-relevant topics — address the problem, not your product
  • Structure: 80% education, 20% product. Never pitch in the first 40 minutes.
  • Promote 3 weeks in advance via email, LinkedIn, and partner channels
  • Follow up with attendees within 24 hours — segment hot leads (attended 45+ min) for direct outreach
  • Repurpose recordings into blog posts, clips, and gated assets

Field Events & Conferences

  • Focus on 2–3 high-quality conferences per year where your ICP is concentrated — not every industry event
  • Pre-schedule meetings before the event — walk-by traffic rarely converts
  • Measure success by meetings booked at event, not booth traffic or badge scans
  • Sponsored events: only worth the investment if you can track meeting-booked rate from the event
  • Follow-up within 24 hours: personalized email referencing the specific conversation, not a generic nurture sequence

Deep-Dive: Partner / Channel

Partner channels produce the highest-quality leads because referred customers arrive pre-qualified by someone who understands both your product and the buyer's needs. The challenge is building and activating a partner ecosystem.

🤝
Partner & Channel Referrals
Best for: companies with natural ecosystem — SI partners, platform partners, or complementary products with the same ICP

Partner Types

TypeHow It WorksRevenue ModelLIR Implication
Referral Partners Partner sends qualified leads, you close and retain 10–20% referral fee per closed deal Very Low — partner pre-qualifies ICP fit
Resellers Partner sells and manages the customer relationship 20–40% revenue share Medium — depends on partner's CS quality
Technology Partners Integration creates mutual referral flow from shared user base Co-marketing, co-sell Low — integration users have higher adoption

Key Success Factors

  • Partners must understand your ICP and LIR framework — they are selling on your behalf. Invest in partner enablement as heavily as rep enablement.
  • Partner qualification matters. A poorly qualified referral from a partner is worse than no referral — it poisons the relationship and wastes CS resources.
  • Partner incentives: tiered commission (higher tiers unlock higher rates), co-marketing budget for joint campaigns, technical certification programs that increase partner confidence
  • Track LIR by partner. If a specific partner's referrals consistently underperform on LIR, address it with the partner — their qualification criteria may be misaligned.

Building a Multi-Channel Demand Gen Machine (Stage 3)

Stage 3 is about controlled diversification — adding channels sequentially, each with its own validated LIUE before scaling. Never add a new channel before the current channel is stable.

Stage 2
Prove 1 Channel
Validate cost per SQL, LIR rate, and LIUE for your primary channel. Hit 3× LIUE before moving on.
Transition
Add 2nd Channel
Maintain primary channel at scale. Run 60-day test on new channel with fixed budget and clear success thresholds.
Stage 3 Early
Validate + Add 3rd
Validate 2nd channel hits LIUE threshold. Add 3rd channel. Begin multi-touch attribution modeling.
Stage 3 Mature
Portfolio Management
Quarterly LIUE review by channel. Reallocate budget from low-LIUE channels to high-LIUE channels every quarter.

Attribution Model

Use multi-touch attribution: first touch (channel that generated initial awareness), last touch (channel that generated the SQL), and linear mid-touch (channels that influenced the buyer during evaluation). Track all three to understand the full funnel contribution of each channel.

Channel Health Check

Track LIUE by channel quarterly. Ask: is the LTV/CAC ratio maintaining above 3 for each channel? Channels that drop below 3 need investigation before budget increases. Channels that drop below 1 must be paused immediately.
